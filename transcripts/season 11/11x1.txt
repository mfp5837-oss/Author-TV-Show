Swept Away
Introduction
Arthur walks onto the balcony of a clock tower and stands beside the large clockface.

Arthur: Have you ever noticed how different people have different senses of time? Like D.W. Sometimes she thinks a minute is like an hour.

Flashback: D.W., Kate and Arthur sit in the backseat of a car.

D.W.: Are we there yet?

Arthur: D.W., that's the eighth time you've asked. You can't ask again for at least ten minutes.

Kate: (giggles)

D.W.: Okay. Are the ten minutes up yet?

Arthur: (groans)

Flashback: Arthur and Buster are sitting in the Sugar Bowl while the waitress is taking their orders.

Arthur: Then there's Buster time, which is closely linked to his stomach.

Buster: I'll have the Harry Mills Meatball Experience.

Arthur: Buster, it's only nine in the morning. It's breakfast time.

Buster: I’ve already had breakfast. This is lunch.

Arthur: But if you have lunch now, you'll be really hungry by dinner.

The waitress brings a large bowl of pickles.

Buster: Not if I have dinner at lunch. And tonight I'll get a head start on tomorrow's breakfast.

He offers Arthur a pickle.

Arthur: Eew!

Buster: I'm just a meal ahead of you. Mmm! Pickles!

He bites into the pickle.

The flashbacks end.

Arthur: Sometimes, it seems like time is going really fast. Arthur grabs hold of the big clock hand which is now moving very fast. (fast:) Like when you go to an amusement park with all your friends and the day just flies by and everything's over before you know it!

Mr. Ratburn looks out of a window above the clock and hands Arthur two sheets of paper.

Mr. Ratburn: You have ten minutes to complete this test.

The hand now moves very slowly.

Arthur: Sometimes it goes really slowly (slowly:) like when you take a test and don't know the answers. He crumples up the paper and throws it away. Then he jumps back on the balcony. But there's one thing that never changes about time: You can't stop it. Or can you? He holds on to the big clock hand. (grunts) Is it working? I don't know how long I've... (grunts)

The clock hand vibrates and bends at the end.

The screen fades to black.



Title Card: Arthur Swims
A beach is shown. There is a lifeguard with a red flag attached to her high seat.

Arthur, D.W. and Buster run out of a beach house and onto the beach.

Kids: Beach!

The lifeguard blows her whistle and points to the red flag.

Buster: Aw. Red flag. That means we can't swim because the ocean's too rough.

They look at the ocean, which is indeed fairly rough.

Arthur: I'm sure it will change in a little bit.

A while later, Arthur looks at the red flag and the lifeguard through binoculars. The other kids are playing cards with Grandma Thora.

Buster: Well, anything yet?

Arthur: No, she's just...wait a minute! She's reaching for something green... The lifeguard takes a green handkerchief out of her bag and blows her nose. Oh, false alarm, it was just a handkerchief.

D.W.: When is the flag going to change? You said it would be a little bit. It's been hours!

Buster: What if it's like this forever because of the green mouse gases in the atmosphere?

Arthur: It's greenhouse gases, Buster.

Buster: Oh! Is that where the green mice live?

Mrs. Read puts sunscreen on D.W.

D.W.: This is a terrible vacation! Why couldn't we go to Paris like Emily?

Grandma Thora: You know what I used to do when I couldn't swim? Build a sandcastle. Once we built one as big as a bed. It was even in the newspaper.

D.W.: Okay, but Arthur has to dig. I don't like sand under my nails.

Buster: Come on, who knows, we might find some buried treasure.

D.W. draws a large building plan in the sand with a stick.

D.W.: Okay. We'll put the princess' bedroom here and her swimming pool here. Oh, and don't forget the stables for the unicorns!

Arthur: D.W.! This is not going to be a fairy princess castle!

Buster: Yeah, it's Dark Bunny's ice fortress!

Arthur: But it's all sand! It should be the citadel on Saturn from episode 38.

Buster: But that was destroyed in episode 112. Hey, let's do something from episode 331.

Arthur: You mean the “Cave of Crab Men”?

Buster: No, no. Dr. Origami's paper palace! Remember those terrifying swans?

D.W. sits in the sand with a small shovel looking annoyed.

D.W.: Fine, we can build whatever you want. Let's just start.

She starts digging.

Arthur and Buster dig a moat.

D.W. pours water in a bowl of sand. Buster makes mud.

Buster: (laughs)

He passes it to Arthur, who starts building a castle.

Arthur and Buster dig a tunnel into the castle’s base from opposite sides. Their hands touch.

Arthur: (gasps)

Buster: Ooah!

Pal jumps on the castle and destroys it.

Arthur: Ah!

Buster: (gasps)

D.W. has set up Sharky, the inflatable shark, upright against a stick. Pal knocks it over and pulls out the plug.

D.W.: (gasps)

D.W. ties Pal's leash to Mr. Read’s deck chair.

Later, the lifeguard sets up a green flag and blows her whistle.

Mrs. Read: It's a green flag! You can go in now.

Arthur: Ah, let's just finish this tower.

Buster+D.W.: Okay!

They resume digging.

The sun is low in the sky. The kids are putting the finishing touches on the sandcastle. Buster hands Arthur seashells.

Buster: Here. I found some more doors.

Arthur puts two shells on the castle’s door. The grown-ups are packing up.

Mrs. Read: Come on, kids, time to go!

D.W.: Just a sec, I've got to feed the seahorse.

She puts down some kelp next to a dead seahorse and pets it.

Grandma Thora: Sweet Petunia! That is one of the best sandcastles I have ever seen.

Buster: What should we call it?

D.W.: Princess Misty's Mermaid Mansion.

Arthur+Buster: No!

Buster holds up a starfish.

Buster: Hey, look! We could call it the Starfish Enterprise.

D.W.: Enterprise? I can't even say that word!

Arthur: You just said it.

D.W.: Well I don't like it. I say we call it Castle Starfish.

Arthur: Okay, Castle Starfish it is.

Buster nods. Arthur puts the starfish on the top tower of the castle.

That night, Arthur and Buster sleep in a bunk bed in a hotel room. Arthur has the lower bunk.

Buster: (snores)

D.W. comes in.

D.W.: Arthur!

Arthur: Ah! Yeah? He puts on his glasses. D.W., what are you doing up? It's the middle of the night.

D.W.: I couldn't sleep. Grandma snores.

Arthur takes earplugs out of his ears and gives them to D.W.

Arthur: Oh, here. You can borrow my earplugs. Now go to bed.

D.W.: I've been thinking of what we should add to Castle Starfish. Princess Misty needs a guesthouse for the pony.

Arthur: It's not a princess castle. Anyway, it'll probably be gone by tomorrow.

D.W.: Why?

Arthur: Because the tide will have come in and washed it away.

D.W.: What's a tide?

Arthur: It's the movement of the ocean. At certain times of the day the water comes farther up the beach. Take my word for it, that castle is just a heap of sand now.

D.W. walks away looking sad.

The next day the kids run to the beach. Except for one side of the outer wall the castle is still standing.

D.W.: Ha! See, Mr. Pessimister?!

Buster: It's a miracle!

He runs to the castle. The others follow.

Arthur: It's not a miracle, Buster. We must have built it right above the high-tide mark.

D.W.: But I chose the spot and I didn't even know what a tide was.

Buster: She's got a point!

D.W.: See? Some people say the glass is half full, other people just have glasses.

Arthur starts digging.

Arthur: We should make the moat bigger, just in case the tide comes in farther. Come on, let's get started!

Later, Mrs. Read takes a photo of the kids in front of the castle.

Mrs. Read: There! When we get home we'll download it onto the computer. Well, time to wash up for dinner.

Buster: We'll be right there. There's something I have to do first.

He walks into the water and pours lemonade from a can into it.

Arthur: What are you doing?

Buster: Giving half of my lemonade to the ocean as an offering so it won't destroy the castle.

D.W.: Here! And have a cookie.

She throws one into the water. A wave washes it at Arthur’s feet. Arthur picks it up.

Arthur: D.W.! That's littering. I know a much better way we can protect Castle Starfish.

Arthur and Buster carry a wooden board and set it up as a barrier in front of the sandcastle.

Arthur: There. Now it's safe.

That night, Arthur is asleep.

In his dream, the castle is huge and inhabited by all sorts of sea creatures. We zoom down to a dragon bus where snails get picked up and dropped off. The bus zooms away. As a crowd cheers, Arthur arrives walking onto a balcony wearing a crown and holding a trident. The Castle Starfish theme plays.

Arthur: Citizens of Castle Starfish, as your king I am pleased to announce that construction of our new moat is complete.

Crowd: (cheer)

Buster joins Arthur wearing a conch shell as a hat.

Buster: And as your other king, I hereby reinstate casual Fridays. Snails may go to work without wearing their shells.

Crowd: (cheer again)

Confetti rains down. Buster cheers with the crowd.

A seahorse swims up to Arthur and neighs.

Arthur: What's he saying?

The horse neighs.

Buster: Aha. Oh! Hmm. I haven't a clue! Fetch the seahorse whisperer!

The Castle Starfish theme plays for the final time. D.W., wearing a dress of seaweed and a cowboy hat approaches the seahorse. It neighs to her.

D.W.: Aha? Yeah? Then what? You don't say! Okay, good job Seabiscuit. There's a tidal wave headed this way!

Crowd: (scream)

Arthur: Everybody get to the shelters! This is not a drill!

Buster: Quick! To the control tower!

Crowd: (scream again)

On top of a tower Arthur pulls at a lever while a huge wave hits the first houses around the castle.

Arthur+Buster: (strain)

D.W.: It's getting closer! What's taking you guys so long?

Arthur: It's stuck!

Buster: Wait, I see the problem.

He removes half a cookie from the contraption.

D.W.: Oh...whoops.

Arthur: D.W., I told you not to litter.

Arthur and Buster pull the lever and a wooden wall rises up and blocks the wave. The sea is now calm and the colors red, orange, yellow, and green on a rainbow appear.

Arthur: We did it! We're safe!

Crowd: (cheer for the final time)

Buster eats the cookie. Arthur waves to the crowd and thanks them very much.

Crowd: (chanting) Arthur! Arthur! (chanting fades out)

The dream ends. Buster is standing beside Arthur’s bed.

Buster: Arthur! Arthur! Arthur!

Arthur: Huh?

Buster: You better come quick.

Arthur, Buster and D.W. run to the beach. The wooden board has fallen over and about half of the castle has fallen in.

D.W.: It's almost all gone!

Buster: I knew I should have given all my lemonade. Oh, I am so selfish!

Arthur: We'll fix it!

He runs onto the beach.

Buster: What?

D.W.: But there's nothing left to fix.

Arthur moves the board.

Arthur: (strains) We'll need something taller than this and we'll need to dig a deeper moat. Come on! We don't have much time! He starts digging with a small shovel. Buster and D.W. just stand there. We're going home today, we can't leave the castle like this.

Buster and D.W. exchange looks, then they smile and join Arthur.

Arthur builds a moat. It is quickly destroyed by a wave, but he keeps on digging.

Buster fixes a castle wall when a crab crawls out of it and causes the castle to collapse.

Buster: Ah!

D.W. tries to put a twig on a tower, but a gull snatches it away.

Arthur makes a tower with a bucket, but the tower immediately collapses.

A wave destroys Buster’s towers.

Buster: (gasps) It's no use, Arthur, too much water's coming in.

Arthur: We need something big to block the waves. Uh, I know. We'll use our surfboards.

Buster: I don't wanna leave my board here.

Arthur: But it's the only way to save the castle.

D.W.: It's not going to save it forever.

Arthur: It could protect it for a day or two, and then we can come back with better tools and reinforce the walls...

Grandma Thora calls from the wooden stairs.

Grandma Thora: The car is almost all packed up. You kids ready to hit the road?

Arthur: Can't we stay for one more day?

Grandma Thora: I don't think so, dear. You'll build another one next summer and it will be even better.

Arthur: No, it won't.

Mr. Read gets into his delivery van which is parked by the beach. Arthur watches a wave take the rest of Castle Starfish.

Arthur: (sighs)

He gets into the van and they drive off.

At the end of summer, Arthur and Buster stand on the beach wearing coats and look at a picture of Castle Starfish that was published in a newspaper.

Arthur: Judging from the picture, Castle Starfish would have been... right here!

Buster: It wasn't this far from the lifeguard chair. It was that way.

D.W.: No, it was here! It's the back of Princess Misty's throne.

She picks up a shell.

Arthur: That could be any old shell, D.W. And it wasn't a princess castle!

Buster: Maybe it was over there. That sand kind of looks familiar.

He digs with his hands.

Arthur: (sighs)

Mrs. Read waves from the car.

Mrs. Read: Come on, kids, it's chilly! I don't want any of you starting school with a cold!

Arthur: I guess we'll never know exactly where it was.

Buster: At least we have a record of it.

He points to the newspaper. They start walking toward the parking lot.

Arthur: Yeah. It was a pretty good summer, wasn't it?

D.W.: Good summer? Are you kidding? It was the best summer!

She takes out a necklace made of a dried starfish, then she follows the boys.
