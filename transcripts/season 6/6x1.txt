Introduction

Children: (cheer)

Arthur: Sue Ellen is playing this totally amazing new computer game we found on the Web. It's called Virtual Goose. He steps into a glass tube, and is teleported onto the computer screen. The more balls you get in, the more points you get. Blackbelt 44, that's Sue Ellen's game name. She's playing against Suntzu 49. Since we’re playing over the internet, SunTzu 49 could be anywhere in the world. Even China.

Chinese Girl: (mutters in Chinese) The word “Game Over” appears on the screen. (gripes in Chinese)

Arthur: Well, it looks like Blackbelt 44 has done it again.

He disappears. The Chinese girl turns around.

Chinese Girl: Huh?

Arthur reappears in Muffy’s game room.

Kids: (cheer)

Sue Ellen: Yes! I am unbeatable! I'm the queen of the virtual world!

D.W.: I was hoping it wouldn't come to this, Arthur, but you leave me no choice. She pulls a scrap of paper out of her dress. Recognize this?

Arthur: No.

D.W.: Well, you should. It's your name written on your napkin. Remember last week, when I gave you my TV time?

Flashback: Arthur is lying on the couch with a sandwich, popcorn and a drink.

Marc Brown (on TV): Well, actually, the Bionic Bunny...

D.W.: You wanted to watch some dopey show about a man who made Bionic Bunny.

Marc Brown (on TV): ...studying children and the effects of super-heroes on their lives.

Arthur: No, I'm not!

D.W.: Well, I have to be sure you're not lying. Sign this napkin.

Marc Brown (on TV): ...and when he goes to work at the television studio...

D.W.: Well, it's later now, and it's time to pay up! Pay up!

Arthur: Aw. I know! What if I promise to play with you for two whole hours next weekend?

D.W.: Make it three, and you've got a deal!

She hands him the napkin and he signs.

Kids: (cheer)

Sheep: (bleats)

Scientist: Yes. Excellent!

Sue Ellen: We'll just see about that! Out of my way!

Sue Ellen: Just one more game.

Children: Aww.

Buster: Hey, Francine, check out my new form of exercise. It's called Earobics! He starts moving his ears. A-one and two, a-one and two...

Francine: (laughs) In Sue Ellen’s imagination Francine turns into a goose. Honk! Honk! Honk! Sue Ellen uses her fork to flick peas at Francine. Grr!

***
