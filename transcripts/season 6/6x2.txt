Introduction
Brain, Buster, Binky and Arthur are sitting around a campfire in the woods.

Buster: Aah, this is the life. A warm fire, good friends, and three pounds of Beef Jerky...

He eats some. Something rustles in the bushes.

Arthur: What was that?!

Buster: M-m-maybe it was a lion!

Brain: Buster, there are no lions in North America. More rustling. Except for, of course, the m-mountain lions!

Mr. Ratburn comes.

Mr. Ratburn: Now, just stay calm, everyone!

Binky: Where's the escape button? I wanna quit the game!

Arthur: Binky, this isn't a game! This is real!

More rustling.

Kids: (gasp)

Title Card: Bird's Egg
In the Powers’ ice-cream shop Brain is preparing a cone with what looks like fish fins.

Brain: Who ordered the Dolce di Pescaros?

Buster: Oh, I did!

Arthur: What's that?

Buster: French vanilla and fresh fish!

Arthur: Ugh!

Buster: Mmm! You can really taste the cod!

Brain hands Arthur a clothes peg which he puts on his nose. Brain is already wearing one.

Arthur: Thanks.

Sue Ellen comes in.

Sue Ellen: Hi, guys! Shouldn't you be playing Virtual Goose? I thought the final match of the play-offs to the elimination round of the nineteenth championship was today.

Arthur: It is, but since D.W. started playing, Goose has got boring. She always wins.

Buster: Besides, who wants to spend all their time inside playing computer games anyway?

Muffy comes in.

Muffy: Guess what! I just downloaded Virtual Goose, version 5.0! It's so cool!

Arthur: 5.0, wow!

Buster: It must be so good, they skipped versions 2 through 4! I wanna play right now.

Arthur: Me too!

Brain: Let's go! They all run out. Sorry, Sue Ellen, we're closed!

He turns the “Open” sign on the door around.

Sue Ellen: (sighs) Addicts!

***

The introduction of the video game starts with a Star Wars-inspired opening crawl.

Video Game Narration: In a time before time, in a place so remote it has never been seen before by upright mammals, the virtual goose laid six eggs. We see a nest in a palm tree. The geese break out of their eggs. They were Sporty Goose, Stylish Goose, Strong Goose, Silly Goose...

Silly Goose: Yuck yuck yuck.

Video Game Narration: ...Smart Goose, and finally just Plain Goose. Because of hard times, the Virtual Goose had to leave her six gifted children and pursue a career in online gaming. The mother goose flies off. She vowed to return with fame and fortune, but before she could, an evil wind blew the nest out of the tree. Now these geese must fend for themselves alone in the deep dark woods. Only one will survive. Choose a goose, pass the trials, and see if you can be “The Best of the Nest”.

The threatening shadow of a cloud falls on the geese. They run off in different directions.

The introduction ends. A split screen shows six kids choosing their goose.

Francine: Sporty!

Muffy: Stylish!

Binky: Strong!

Buster: Silly!

Brain: Smart!

Arthur: Aw. I guess I have to be just Plain Goose.

Brain plays with Smart Goose.

Computer: Level one. You are faced with a rushing river. Do you: A) Fly across the river? B) Wait for a passing log? or C) Do the hokey-pokey?

Brain: Hmm... A!

Smart Goose falls in the river and is eaten by a shark. A tombstone with the words “Game Over” appears.

Computer: Game Over.

Brain: What?!

***

Brain takes a book from a shelf in the library. George is working on a library computer with Binky standing behind his chair.

Binky: Haven't you finished your research yet? I've got a game to play!

George leaves and Binky sits down. Brain goes to Binky and shows him the book.

Brain: You see? Judging from its size my goose should have been able to fly!

Binky: If that's true, then why aren't you still in the game?

Brain: Because it's a dumb game, that's why! I mean, a shark in a river? Come on!

Muffy sits nearby and plays on her laptop.

Muffy: Don't listen to him, Binky. The Brain's just upset, because he couldn't last one day in the wild!

Brain: I could, too.

Muffy: Well, I guess we'll never know, will we? (gasps) It's the third trial!

Stylish Goose has found some mushrooms on an ice floe.

Computer: You have come to a patch of mushrooms. Do you: A) Avoid the mushrooms? B) Eat the mushrooms? or C) Do the hokey-pokey?

Muffy: Eat the mushrooms!

Stylish Goose eats the mushrooms.

Computer: Congratulations. You have passed on to level four.

Brain: But that's ridiculous! They could've been poisonous!

Muffy: Spoilsport!

***

Brain shows a sign-up list for a camping weekend to Buster, Arthur and Francine.

Brain: A real weekend camping in the real woods. Are you up to the challenge?

Buster: Gee, I don't know. Will there be real food?

Brain: My dad makes amazing s'mores.

Buster: Where do I sign?

He signs. Muffy is behind him at her locker.

Muffy: A camping trip? Puh-lease! No showers, no beds, no TV? Ha. No, thanks! Er, I mean, I'd love to go, but I can't because of my fragile health. (coughs) You four have a good time.

She leaves.

Francine: I know what Muffy's trying to do. She wants to get rid of us so it'll be easier for her to become the Best of the Nest.

Arthur: Imagine what she'll be like if she wins.

In his imagination Muffy is in the cafeteria with Arthur, Buster, and Francine.

Muffy: First there were six, then there was one. Muffy Crosswire, numero uno, top bird, the nesterator...

Francine: Pass the salt, please.

Muffy: Sporty Goose wants the salt. Will Stylish Goose give it to her? Negativo. Salt is only for the best. Egg honcho of the honkers, triple crown of the eiderdown, commander of the gander...

Arthur+Francine: Aw!

The fantasy ends. Buster scratches out his name and gives the list back to Brain.

Buster: Sorry, Brain.

Francine: Yeah, we'd love to go, but it wouldn't be fair to the rest of the class.

***

Brain talks to Sue Ellen, who is wearing karate clothes and practicing with a wooden board in her back yard.

Sue Ellen: Sorry, Brain, I've got a karate tournament this weekend. Yee-ha! She attempts to chop the board with her hand edge. She fails and seems to be in pain. Make...that a...trip to the doctor's!

She runs to the house.

***

Prunella sits in the ice-cream shop playing with a cootie catcher.

Prunella Camping, huh? Sorry, Brain, it says no travelling on days with the letter Y in them.

***

Mr. Ratburn is cleaning the blackboard while the kids walk into the classroom.

Brain: Hm. Friday already, and still no takers. This camping trip's a complete dud.

Mr. Ratburn: A camping trip?! I so enjoyed camping when I was a boy. In the woods, with the awesome beauty of nature....

A flashback shows a perhaps ten-year-old Mr. Ratburn walking through the woods with a field book.

Young Mr. Ratburn: That's Acer rubrum. That's Ulmus americana. Oh, what's that?! Moth pupae!

The flashback ends.

Mr. Ratburn: I'd love to go!

He signs Brain’s list.

Brain: Great.

He gives a fake smile.

***

The bell rings and the students leave the classrooms.

Mr. Ratburn: Have a good weekend, class! Work on those copper smelting dioramas. Oh, Alan, I have some wonderful books here I thought we'd take with us. There's Grimples's “A History of Elwood City’s Environs from the Pleistocene to the Present” and “Trail Maps of the Western Hemisphere”. Very handy if we get lost. Oh, and this little morsel: “1001 Low-fat Recipes for Bark”.

Brain: Um, those sound really interesting, but I bet we could print out that information off the internet. It'd be easier to carry.

Mr. Ratburn: The... internet? Well, you see, hem, I’ve never actually, er, skied the webbing of the information freeway, as it were.

Brain: It's as easy as tying your shoes.

***

Mr. Ratburn sits at the classroom computer and taps the monitor with the mouse. Brain smiles and shakes his head. He shows how to use a mouse. Mr. Ratburn opens the disk tray.

Mr. Ratburn: Ooh!

Mr. Ratburn plays solitaire. Brain looks bored and moves the mouse on “Quit”.

***

That night, Mr. Ratburn is still at the computer and Brain looks tired.

Computer: Welcome.

Brain: Okay, we're finally on the Internet. Now, for trail maps, we can go to...

Mr. Ratburn: Oh, chat rooms! Oh, the No-strings-attached Bunraku Puppetry Discussion Group!

Brain: It's getting kind of late, Mr. Ratburn. Maybe we should look for some stuff on camping.

Mr. Ratburn ignores him and types.

Mr. Ratburn: Philistine! The shamisen is not a four-stringed Japanese lute. That's the biwa. This is amazing, Alan. Why, I could surf the web all weekend!

Brain: (sighs)

He scratches Mr. Ratburn’s name from the list.

***

Brain sits in his room and starts a long row of dominoes. The last domino causes a small rocket to fly up and hit a nutcracker which cracks an almond, which Brain eats. His dad comes in.

Mr. Powers: You know, Alan, it's not too late. We can still go on the trip, just you and me.

Brain: Thanks, dad. I kind of wanted to go with my friends, but everyone's too busy with their computers.

Mr. Powers: Oh, that reminds me. I've got to update the ice-cream shop's website.

He leaves.

The doorbell rings and Brain opens. Mr. Ratburn stands outside wearing explorer’s clothing and carrying a landing net and a canoe.

Mr. Ratburn: Look what I bought online!

***

Brain gets into Mr. Ratburn’s car looking unhappy. The canoe is tied to the roof.

Mr. Ratburn: Before we set out on our adventure, I just have to make a few brief stops.

The car stops in front of the Read house where Arthur is waiting with a large backpack.

Brain: Arthur, you're coming. But I thought you were going to play “Best of the Nest” this weekend.

Arthur: I was, but on level ten my goose was destroyed by a cruel force of nature.

Flashback: Arthur is playing Best of the Nest.

Computer: Night has fallen and the temperature is dropping. Do you: A) Wrap yourself in dead leaves? B) Cuddle up with the crocodile? or C) Do the hokey-pokey?

Arthur: Hm... It must be A! The telephone rings. Arthur leaves the room and D.W. comes. You hear the computer burbling. Arthur comes back. No!

The screen shows a well-fed crocodile next to Plain Goose’s glasses. It burps out some feathers.

The flashback ends.

Arthur: That little goose never had a chance against D.W.

***

In front of the Baxters’ condo, Buster puts his stuff in the trunk and gets into the car.

Buster: My mom made me stop playing, because she said staring at the computer for so long was bad for my eyes. Isn't that crazy? He sees Mr. Ratburn. Hey, Prunella, when did you learn to drive?

***

Binky puts his stuff in the trunk.

Binky: Miss Turner said I was hogging the library's computer. He gets into the car. Hey, Brain, do you think it will be cold this summer?

Brain: Probably not. Why do you ask?

Binky: Because my dad said he'd buy me a computer when it snows in July.

Mr. Ratburn: All right, that's the last of us. Now it's off to the mountains!

He drives off.

Brain: What about Francine and Muffy?

***

Muffy plays at her computer. She looks tired out.

Muffy: Must...survive... Her phone rings. Hello?

Francine is on the other end, looking equally tired.

Francine: You know I'm gonna win. My fingers are faster.

Muffy: Well, my computer is faster.

Francine: Give up!

Muffy: Never!

She hangs up.

Computer: Congratulations! You have passed on to level 87!

Muffy: Ohh...

***

Just as in the introduction, Brain, Buster, Binky and Arthur are sitting around a campfire in the woods.

Buster: This is the life. A warm fire, good friends, and three pounds of Beef Jerky...

He eats some. Something rustles in the bushes.

Arthur: What was that?!

Buster: M-m-maybe it was a lion!

Brain: Buster, there are no lions in North America. More rustling. Except for, of course, the m-mountain lions!

Mr. Ratburn comes.

Mr. Ratburn: Now, just stay calm, everyone!

Binky: Where's the escape button? I wanna quit the game!

Arthur: Binky, this isn't a game! This is real!

More rustling.

Kids: (gasp)

Bailey comes out of the bushes carrying a huge backpack.

Bailey: Mr. Ratburn, I presume. (gasps for air)

He falls over exhausted. Muffy comes out of the bushes talking into her cell phone.

Muffy: We made it, Daddy. We made it, and the reception is fantastic!

Francine follows her.

***

The campers are toasting marshmallows on the fire. Mr. Ratburn offers food.

Mr. Ratburn: Elm bark with lichen tapenade?

Brain: So which of you wound up being Best Of The Nest?

Muffy: Neither of us.

Francine: Yeah. Who knew that the way to scare off a bear is to do the hokey-pokey.

Binky: The hokey-pokey? That's just stupid!

Buster: (laughs)

A loud growling is heard from the woods.

All: Ahhh!

They exchange looks, then they start doing the hokey-pokey.

Binky: (sings:) "You put your left foot in, you put your left foot out."

Kids: (sing:) "You put your left foot in and then shake it all about. You do the hokey pokey and you turn yourselves around. Mr. Ratburn joins them. That’s what it’s all about."
