Introduction
A view of Istanbul is shown. In a side street is a with a sign “Bakkal Mahmut” [Grocery Store Mahmut]. A man is sweeping the sidewalk. He looks at his watch.

Adil's Father: Adil! (speaks Turkish)

Upstairs, window shutters are opened and Adil looks out.

Adil: (speaks Turkish) He looks at the camera. Oh, hello. You probably don't understand Turkish, so I'll speak in English. That's my father. And that's our store behind him.

Adil's Father: Hello. Welcome. Have a good day.

Adil: Please, come in.

Adil sits on his bed. Another boy rolls up a sleeping bag.

Onder: Hi, I'm Onder, Adil's best friend. But not for long.

Adil: Onder didn't do his maths homework. He thinks our teacher, Mr. Yuksel, is going to throw him into the Sea of Marmara.

Onder: And I can't even swim!

Adil’s little sister comes in.

Ayse: Adil! (shouts in Turkish) She looks into the camera. Merhaba!

Adil steps in front of her.

Adil: Pay no attention to her. She's not supposed to be here.

Ayse holds up a toy lamb.

Ayse: Look! Adil's little lambie! Heeheeheehee!

Adil: Ayse! Ayse runs out. Okay, I admit it. She's my little sister. But I'm not gonna talk about the stuffed lamb! Whenever Ayse drives me crazy, there's only one person I really want to talk to.

He sits down at the computer and logs in.

Onder: Adil! Ask him to ask his friend, The Head, if he knows the answers to numbers five, seven and nine.

Adil: Onder, his friend's name is not The Head!

An icon shows that Adil has mail. Adil smiles.

Title Card: Arthur Swims
Several months earlier, Arthur and his Dad search the workshop.

Mr. Read: Any sign of my olive depitter yet?

Arthur: No.

Mr. Read: Great. This tapenade will take me hours to make!

Arthur: But I did find these letters. Cool stamps!

D.W.: Who are they from? Arthur's real parents?

Mr. Read: They're from Tatsuo Matsumoto. He takes the letters. He was a pen pal from Japan I wrote to when I was in the 7th Grade. I remember this one! Tatsuo got lost in Kyoto at the Obon Festival. Haha! Ah, I forgot how much I enjoyed corresponding with Tatsuo.

Arthur: Can I read them?

An olive hits Mr. Read. D.W. holds the depitter.

D.W.: Uh-oh! I found it!

***

Arthur reads Tatsuo’s letter lying on his bed.

He is still reading later that evening.

Arthur: (laughs)

The next morning he wakes up surrounded by letters.

***

Mr. Ratburn is clapping erasers over the waste paper basket. Arthur approaches him.

Arthur: Mr. Ratburn... (coughs) Do you know how I can get a pen pal?

Mr. Ratburn: A pen pal? Yes, of course. In fact, I just recently earmarked a web spot for teachers that facilitates such things. They walk over to the computer. Now, where is that switch?

Arthur turns the computer on.

***

Arthur sits on Buster’s bed with a printed list with several addresses. Buster is working on a model ship at his desk.

Arthur: Adil Akyuz. Eight-years-old, from Turkey. Hey, Buster, do you know anything about Turkey?

Buster: Sure, there's honey-glazed turkey and smoked turkey and my favorite: roast turkey. He takes a bone out of his food collection. Oh, this is from my very first Thanksgiving. You wanna hold it?

Arthur: No! Ach. I'm talking about the country.

Buster: Oh. Hey, I know a lot about that too! That's where “Illinois Jack and The Caravan of Horrors” takes place.

Buster opens a comic book. An Arabian bazaar is shown. Illinois Jack and Mustafa are running from a large mechanical camel.

Buster (narrator): Illinois Jack has this really cool Turkish friend in it named Mustafa. This is where Mustafa and Illinois get chased through the Covered Bazaar.

Illinois Jack: Just a bit longer, buddy.

Mustafa: Illy, the Caliph's iron camel is gaining on us!

Buster: And then, Mustafa saves him from a pit of man-eating newts.

Illinois Jack lies at the bottom of a pit surrounded by newts. Mustafa lets down a rope.

Mustafa: I will not let you die, Illy! Ah!

He disappears and is replaced by Nazi soldiers.

Buster: And then... Ho-ho! This is my favorite part... The banquet!

Illinois Jack and Mustafa are sitting at a huge table in a tent.

Mustafa: Lamb's eyes, Illy?

Jack sees a plate of eyes and looks disgusted.

The fantasy ends.

Buster: You should write to that Turkish kid, Arthur. I bet he's really exciting.

Arthur underlines Adil’s name.

***

Adil and Onder are sitting on Adil’s bed. Adil reads Arthur’s letter.

Adil: “…and then I had a slice of pepperoni pizza with my friend Buster at the mall.”

Onder: What's...pepperoni?

Adil: I don't know. What's a mall? “That was my day. Please tell me all about your day and life in Turkey. Sincerely, Arthur Read.”

He starts writing while Onder looks over his shoulder.

***

The Read family is having dinner. Arthur has Adil’s letter beside his plate.

Arthur: And then on weekends, Adil works in his father's grocery store. They sell all sorts of cool things like spices and, and lamb's eyes...!

D.W.: They sell lamb's eyes in the grocery store?

Arthur: Well, he didn't say that, but that's what they eat in “Illinois Jack and the Caravan of Horrors”.

D.W.: You're leaving out the best part. He has a little sister and Arthur's going to ask her to get me a Mary Moo Cow CD in Turkish.

Arthur: D.W., they don't have Mary Moo Cow in Turkey.

D.W.: How do you know?

Mr. Read: Did Adil like your letter?

Arthur: Well, he said he liked it, but...

Mr. Read: But what?

Arthur: I don't know... Maybe he was being polite. My letter was dull compared to his.

Mr. Read: I'm sure that isn't the case, Arthur.

D.W.: Yes, it is. Arthur read me part of it and I fell asleep. That letter was like warm milk.

Arthur looks annoyed.

***

Arthur sits at his desk writing a letter. Pal sniffs at crumpled up pages on the floor.

Arthur: Dear Adil. Thank you for your really interesting letter. These are some exciting things that happened to me this week...

While writing, he imagines Adil reading the letter sitting on an oriental bed dressed like a prince from Arabian Nights. A monkey sits beside him. His dad sits in front of a mirror wearing a face.

Adil: "I got an A-minus on a math test and I went to the Sugar Bowl with (yawns) Buster and... Can't...stay...awake...

He and the monkey fall asleep.

Adil’s Father: Adil! What is wrong? He picks up the letter. "...and Francine told a really funny joke. What do you get when you cross a...?"

He falls asleep.

The letter flies out of the tent and past a flock of sheep who promptly fall asleep. A map of Turkey is shown. The letter flies out of Istanbul and across the country. Loud snoring is heard.

The fantasy ends. Arthur looks at his letter.

Arthur: (yawns) This is even putting me to sleep. Oh, well, maybe tomorrow something exciting will happen to me.

He sits down on the bed.

***

It rains. Arthur and Buster sit in the Sugar Bowl playing “basketball” with a cherry and eating utensils.

Buster: And number five dribbles down the field. He cuts left, he cuts right. The goal is open. Oh, he shoots... Hey, you're not shooting.

Arthur: Oh, sorry, Buster. I was just wondering what I'm gonna write to Adil. Even less happened today than yesterday!

Buster: Speak for yourself. I've eaten 27 cherries! That's my record.

Arthur: Can I use that?

Buster: Sure. Hey, didn't Adil ask you lots of questions in the last letter?

Arthur: I already answered them... A mall is a big building with lots of stores. Pepperoni is a type of sausage. They sound so boring. Maybe I should just leave them out.

Buster: Pepperoni's not boring!

Arthur: (sighs)

Buster: Why don't you just ask him more question about Turkey. It sounds like such an exciting place. Take Illinois Jack for inspiration.

He hands Arthur the comic.

Arthur writes a letter on his bed with the comic beside him. Pal brings a chew bone and looks expectantly.

Arthur: “Dear Adil. What is your school like? How do you get there?” I wonder if he rides a camel to school like Illy's friend, Mustafa.

A fantasy shows Adil riding to school on a camel. The school lies in a desert and looks like a cross between Lakewood Elementary and a Bedouin tent. Adil gets off the camel and chains it to a bike stand.

Adil: See you at recess, boy.

The fantasy ends.

Arthur: Wow! Wish I had a camel!

***

Adil and Onder sit on Adil’s bed while Adil reads the letter.

Adil: "Does you camel have its own tent, or does it sleep in yours?" I don't have a camel.

Onder: Neither do I. I don't even know anybody who has a camel. Why does he think you have a camel?

Adil shrugs.

Adil: "What is your favorite food? Please describe the taste of... lamb's eyes?!”

He looks shocked.

Onder: Eww! You never told me you ate a lamb's eye.

Adil: I haven't, Onder! I don't know where Arthur's getting these ideas from?

Onder: What are you going to write back?

Adil: I don't know!

***

The mail falls through the letter slot. Mr. Read picks it up.

Arthur: Anything from...?

Mr. Read: Sorry, Arthur.

***

Some other day Mrs. Read brings the mail while Arthur is having breakfast. He eagerly searches for Adil’s letter, but finds none.

Arthur: Oh....

***

Arthur reads Illinois Jack under a tree in the yard. Leaves are falling. Mr. Higgins, the mailman, comes by.

Mr. Higgins: (whistles)

Arthur: Hi, Mr. Higgins. Anything for...?

Mr. Higgins shakes his head.

Arthur looks sad. Alberto comes by with a wooden kendo sword.

Alberto: Hey, is that an Illinois Jack comic?

Arthur: Yeah, I borrowed it from Buster.

Alberto: Ha-ha! These things are so funny.

Arthur: Funny?

Alberto: Yeah, they always get everything wrong. Like, when Illy went to Ecuador, he found a golden Mayan temple. Ha! The Maya were from Mexico!

Arthur: Oh! I thought the comic was accurate.

Alberto: No way! It's like those American TV shows I watched when I was a young kid in Ecuador. They made me think that every kid in the U.S. went surfing after school and then went home to their skyscrapers, and put ketchup on all their food.

People are shown surfing. One rides his surfboard up to the door of a skyscraper. A family is having dinner and a teenage boy puts ketchup on his salad and in his glass.

Alberto: Yep, you would sure have a weird impression of a place, if all you knew of it came from TV and comics.

Arthur looks thoughtful.

***

Mr. Read makes sushi in the kitchen. Arthur stands beside him.

Arthur: So that's why he hasn't written back for three weeks. Because I probably offended him with my questions. Maybe I should just get an American pen pal.

Mr. Read: Why don't you write back to him and explain what happened?

Arthur: Well, I'm kind of embarrassed.

Mr. Read: Give it one more shot. It's not always easy making friends in other cultures, but trust me - it's worth it!

Arthur looks at the list of addresses.

Arthur: What a minute! Adil has an email address?

***

Adil is playing Virtual Goose when he sees that he got an email. He opens it.

Arthur's Voice: "Dear Adil. Just wondering if you got my letters.”

Adil: “PS: I didn't know you had email. How do you plug your computer in??" He smiles and shakes his head. Then he answers. “Dear Arthur. I plug my computer into the wall of our apartment. I don't live in a tent.”

Arthur: "Oh, sorry. I read this comic and it gave me some weird ideas about how you live. Is that why you didn't write back?"

Adil: "Yes. I was going to correct you, but I thought it would take forever. And then you didn't answer any of my questions."

Arthur: "That's because my answers sounded boring. I mean, a mall's just one really big store with lots of little stores in it."

Adil: So that's what it is! “We have lots of those in Istanbul. The biggest and oldest one is the Kapalıçarşı. In English, that means ‘The Covered Bazaar’.”

Arthur: Really? Hey, I sort of live near a covered bazaar.

D.W. comes.

D.W.: Are you playing Virtual Goose? The answer's D - Do the Hokey-Pokey.

Arthur: Ah, D.W., go away! I'm writing to someone!

D.W. runs off.

D.W.: Hokey-Pokey! Hokey-Pokey!

Adil: “Arthur, are you still there?”

Arthur: “Sorry, Adil, my pesky little sister just interrupted me.”

Adil: (laughs) “Ayse is always doing that, and it’s always when I really don't want to be disturbed. How does she know?”

Arthur: “Adil, I think we have a lot more in common than I ever imagined.”

***

The story continues after the introduction. Adil’s father is still cleaning in front of his store.

Onder: Well, was he able to reach The Head? We have to be at school in twenty minutes.

Adil: Arthur's friend's name is Brain, and no, he can't call him now, it's late at night in Elwood City.

He turns off the computer and stands up.

Onder: Oh! Well, what did you two talk about?

Adil: He was mad at me.

Onder: Why?

Adil: I found a Mary Moo Cow CD for D.W.

They leave the room with their school bags.

***

D.W. dances on her bed in pajamas wearing headphones and singing the Mary Moo Cow song in Turkish.

D.W.: “Seni seviyorum, evet çok çok seviyorum. O kadar tatlı, o kadar güzelsin ki. Seni...”

Arthur: D.W., go to sleep! Ahhh.

He covers his head with his pillow. D.W. goes on singing.

D.W.: “...seviyor, evet seviyor, evet seviyor.”
