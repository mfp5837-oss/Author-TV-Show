Introduction
Francine, dressed as a Bedouin, rides a camel through a desert.

Francine: So hungry. So thirsty. She sees palm trees in the distance. An oasis! I'm saved! Come on, girl, head for those trees!

Camel: Ah! My bunions are killing me. If you were in a rush, you should've rented a dune buggy!

Francine: Forget it, I'll walk.

The camel kneels by the oasis. Francine gets off.

Camel: Suit yourself.

Francine: Look at all that juicy, ripe fruit!

She is about to bite into an orange.

Other Francine: Drop that fruit!

Francine: Why?

A cloaked figure steps from behind a palm tree.

Other Francine: Because I said so, that's why.

Francine: Well, that's not good enough. I'm starving! Who are you anyway?

The other person removes the cloak. It is Francine wearing her normal clothes.

Other Francine: You.

Francine: (gasps) But why would I want to keep myself from eating?

The camel eats the orange out of Francine’s hand.

Camel: Don't look at me! This is your meshuggene dream, not mine.

Francine looks bewildered.

Title Card: Francine Bangs Gong
Mrs. Frensky sprays cleaning lotion on the table and polishes it.

Mrs. Frensky: (hums)

Francine lies on the couch.

Francine: I don't understand. Why do we have to clean everything?

Mrs. Frensky: Because this weekend is Yom Kippur, the biggest Jewish holiday of the year. I want things to look perfect.

Catherine hands Francine a feather duster.

Catherine: Here, your highness. Dust!

Francine: Of course I get the hard jobs!

She starts dusting.

Catherine: Are you kidding? You have it easy. You don't have to fast like I do.

Francine: I could fast better than you any day.

Catherine: Oh, yeah? I'd like to see you try it.

Francine: Fine. I will.

Catherine: Oh, please. You don't even know what fasting means.

Francine: So? I don't have to know what it means. I'm still gonna win!

Mrs. Frensky: Calm down, you two. Fasting means going without food all day, and I think you're a little too young to...

The door is heard opening.

Francine+Catherine: Bubby!

Bubby comes in with Mr. Frensky. The girls run up to her and give her a hug.

Bubby Frensky: Oh, my two favorite grandchildren! Come give, give your bubby a hug! Catherine, look at you! Such a beauty! Even though blue is not really your color, darling. And my little Frankeleh. Oh, you have gotten so skinny! Are you feeding her?

Mrs. Frensky: Of course I'm feeding her, Ma!

Bubby Frensky: Don't worry, Frankeleh. Bubby's here. I'll make you some real food.

Francine: Not on Yom Kippur! I'll be fasting with you guys.

Bubby Frensky: The child's fasting? Why?

Francine: I want to! Catherine's doing it.

Mrs. Frensky: (sighs) They grow up so quickly.

Bubby Frensky: Quickly-schmickly. Listen, young lady, don't be in such a rush. Before you know it you'll be old, you'll be eating mashed peas again and looking forward to nothing but nap-time. You want that? Here, give me that. She takes the duster. I see some spots you missed.

***

Arthur, Buster, Francine and Muffy are sitting at a table in the cafeteria.

Muffy: You're not going to eat anything for a whole day?

Francine: Yup! From sunset till sunset the next day.

Muffy: But you can drink water, right?

Francine: No, nothing at all for twenty-five hours.

Buster: I don't think I've gone without food or water for twenty-five minutes!

Arthur: My Turkish pen pal, Adil, also fasts. He does it for a month during Ramadan. Here's his e-mail address.

He gives Francine a piece of paper.

Francine: Thanks. But I doubt, I'll be needing his advice. I'm already in training! This is all I've eaten since seven this morning! She points at a piece of lasagna before her. The schoolbell rings. The kids stand up and Buster takes Francine’s piece of cake. Hey! What are you doing?

Buster: I'm just helping you train!

***

The Reads’ phone rings. Arthur runs downstairs to get it.

Arthur: Hello, Read residence?

The screen is split in the middle, the right side shows Pizza Paula’s.

Paula: Is this Arthur Read? Arthur Read who bought a calzone at Pizza Paula's last Tuesday.

Arthur: Em, yes.

She rings a bell.

Paula: Congratulations! You won first place in the Pizza Paula's Pig Out Raffle!

Arthur: I did? What did I win?

Paula: All the pizza you and your friends can eat, delivered right to your door this Saturday afternoon.

Arthur: No way! Wait, what's the catch?

Paula: There isn't any! Just invite lots of kids so I can photograph them eating my pizza. Is your Mom there?

Arthur: Hold on. Mom!

He runs to get her.

***

Francine sits at her computer.

Computer: “E-mail from Arthur Read.”

Francine: (reads:) “Pizza party at Arthur's house this Saturday. Be there or be square. Or round.” Pizza party! I'm there! She spins on her office chair. Wait! It's on Saturday.

***

Bubby is going through the kitchen cupboards.

Bubby Frensky: No cookies, no pretzels, it's like living in a health food store.

Francine comes in.

Francine: (sniffs)

Bubby Frensky: What's wrong? You look terrible! Are you sick? I'll go get a thermometer.

Francine: I'm fine. It's just that Arthur's having a pizza party and I can't go ‘cause it's on Yom Kippur.

Bubby Frensky: Can't he have it another day? Get me his number. I'll call.

Francine: I already did. The pizzeria said it was too late to pick a new date.

Catherine comes home.

Catherine: A pizza party, hm? Yup, I knew there'd be a reason you couldn't fast on Saturday.

Francine: Well, you're wrong! As long as you're not eating, I'm not eating!

***

The Frenskys are leaving the “Sha’aray Shalom” synagogue.

Francine: That was really cool! I'm actually looking forward to going again tomorrow!

Catherine: Feeling a little...peckish?

Francine: Nope! And according to my stopwatch, I only have.. 22 hours, 13 minutes and 45 seconds to go.

Mr. Frensky: Your mom and I are pretty proud of you, kiddo.

Francine: (blows raspberry)

Catherine rolls her eyes.

***

Francine wakes up at 7 a.m.

Francine: (yawns) Wow! I'm not even hungry! This is gonna be easy.

Catherine sits on her bed and brushes her hair.

Catherine: Oh, yeah! Fasting is a piece of cake. Easy as pie. Or should I say pizza pie?

Francine: I know what you're doing and it's not gonna work.

She walks out.

***

The Frenskys attend a service in the synagogue.

Chasan: (chants:) “Baruch ata Adonai, elohenu melech ha-olam…”

Francine’s Stomach Rumbling

Bubby Frensky: What was that? Is there a dog in here?

Francine: Sorry! It was my stomach.

Bubby Frensky: Oh. to Mrs. Frensky: Listen, I need a little rest. I'll see you at home.

Francine: Francine, why don't you keep her company?

Francine nods. Bubby winks at her. They leave.

***

Francine’s watch says 10:15.

Francine: Eight hours and 44 minutes to go.

She lies down on the living room couch. Then she turns on the TV. A cook is shown taking glazed pecan buns out of the oven.

Announcer (on TV): Glazed pecan buns cool. Mmmm.

Francine changes the channel to a Chickin Lickin’ commercial.

Beauregard Poulet (on TV): Our extra crispy lickin' sticks with Mexicalli cheddarola dip!

Francine changes the channel to a sloppy joe commercial in which a little girl is served a sloppy joe.

Girl (on TV): Sloppy Joes? Mom, you're the best!

Francine turns off the TV. Francine was getting more hungry with all those Food Ads. The TV was torturing her to eat something while she was fasting.

Francine: (swallows) Okay. TV was a bad idea. I know! I'll read!

#

Francine looks at the titles in the bookshelf.

Francine: “Food Of The Gods”, “Breakfast At Tiffany's”, “Who Moved My Cheese”... Aargh! She takes a book. “Little Dorrit”, by Charles Dickens.

***

Francine is reading “Little Dorrit” on the couch.

Francine: “But glimpses were to be caught … roast beef and blisterous Yorkshire pudding, bubbling hot... stuffed fillet of veal… baked potatoes glued together by their own richness?” She closes the book (Sighs) She had enough with all the temptation and being tortured for food, she feels her stomach

(Stomach Rumbling Loudly begging for food). Francine was beyond starving she wishes that she could eat something.

#

Francine sits at her computer.

Francine: (types:) “Dear Adil, Arthur's friend Francine here. Trying to fast till sundown on Yom Kippur. Any advice?”

Adil sits at his computer in Turkey where it is night. Francine reads his answer.

Francine: (reads:) “Hi, Francine. During Ramadan I try to keep my mind off food by being with friends. Why not go visit Arthur?” Hm. They've probably finished the pizza by now. That's a great idea! (types:) “I'll try it. Thanks.”

***

Francine rings Arthur’s doorbell. He opens holding a slice of pizza.

Arthur: Hey! Oh... He hides the pizza behind his back. Um, hi, Francine.

Francine: You don't have to hide your pizza. I'm fine. I just wanted a little company.

Arthur: Okay! Come on in.

***

Muffy, Brain, Buster and Binky are eating pizza in the living room. There are decorations for “Pizza Paula’s”. Francine looks at a pizza.

Francine: (swallows)

Paula: Okay, pizza lovers, let's gather round for some photos. The kids pose with pizza slices. Molto bene! Now, just... Wait a minute!

Francine is looking sad. Paula puts a slice of pizza in her hand.

Francine: But...

Paula: Now we're ready! Okay, kids, say mozzarella cheese!

Kids: Mozzarella cheese!

Paula takes the picture. The kids stare at Francine, who is noisily eating her slice of pizza, Francine stops and stares at the pizza.

***

Francine walks home. Bubby as an angel appears in the clouds.

Bubby Frensky: Eating on Yom Kippur? How could you? You are no longer my little Frankeleh, no, no, no, no, no! From now on, I'm just gonna call you “F” for failure.

Catherine appears as another angel.

Catherine: A-hahaha! I knew you couldn't do it!

Francine: (gasps)

***

Francine walks up to her house. It appears she was only imagining.

Francine: Oh! What have I done?

Francine walks into the apartment past Bubby eating a sandwich.

Francine: Bubby, what are you doing??

Bubby Frensky: What does it look like? Having a sandwich. It's very dry, though. Your mother's got something against mayonnaise?

Francine: But you're breaking the rules!

Bubby Frensky: No, I'm not! The Torah forbids us to fast if it hurts our health. And sweetheart, look at Bubby's body. It's a lemon.

Francine: I wish I had an excuse.

Bubby Frensky: Lemme guess. You ate some of Arthur's pizza, right?

Francine: It's that obvious?

Bubby Frensky: Bubbys know these things. Plus, there's a mushroom on your shirt.

Francine: Oh. I'm a failure!

Bubby Frensky: You're not a failure! Do you know why we Jews celebrate Yom Kippur?

Francine: It's when we ask for forgiveness for all the mistakes we’ve made.

Bubby Frensky: So? What are you, Frankeleh? Perfect?

Francine: I guess not.

Bubby Frensky: Anyway, you're not required to fast until you're thirteen. So, in the meantime, there are more important matters that need your attention.

Francine: Really? Like what?

Bubby Frensky: Maybe you should ask Catherine. She's in the kitchen.

***

Francine runs into the kitchen where Catherine is icing a cake.

Francine: Ah-ha! Busted!

Catherine: I'm not eating cake, squirt, I'm just making it. And it's for you.

Francine: It is?

Catherine: Well, it's for everyone. But I was gonna put your name on it. After all, it is your first Yom Kippur fast!

Francine: You mean it was my first Yom Kippur fast. I stuffed my face full of pizza at Arthur's. Well, aren't you gonna rub my face in it?

Catherine: I didn't make it all the way through my first time either. Wanna lick the spoon?

Francine takes the spoon and licks it.

***

Bubby, Catherine and Francine are sitting on the couch watching TV. Their parents come home.

Mr. Frensky: Kids? Bubby? We're home!

Mrs. Frensky: You must be starving! I know we are. So, guess what we got?

Mr. Frensky: Pizza!

Bubby Frensky: Oh, how wonderful! I am so glad you're not cooking. Paper plates? What, china's suddenly too good for me? Oh, I wish your grandfather were here to see this. He would have loved this. He would have been so proud of you girls.

The episode ends with Bubby as an angel and the caption “Special Appearance by Joan Rivers”.
