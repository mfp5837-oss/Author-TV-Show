Kung Fool Card
Kung Fool
Introduction
Several kids stand in front of the school. Arthur starts walking home.

Arthur: Sometimes you think you know someone, but then they do something that really surprises you. Like D.W. the other night.

Flashback: Mr. Read prepares what looks like green sausages in the kitchen. Arthur sits at the table.

Arthur: What are they?

Mr. Read: Yaprak dolması, a Turkish delicacy. Vine leaves stuffed with rice, lamb and pine nuts.

Arthur: You made something else for D.W., right? She's not going to want those anywhere near her plate.

Mr. Read puts the dish on the table.

D.W.: Hey, little green hot-dogs. She bites into one. Arthur and Mr. Read stare at her. Wait... these don't taste like hot-dogs. They're ten times better! She takes another one. Ah. Mm, delicious! Mm. Can we have these every night?

Mr. Read smiles.

The flashback ends.

Arthur: Or Mr. Haney.

Flashback: In the school auditorium the kids stare open-mouthed at the stage. Mr. Haney stands on one leg, balances one plate on the other foot and another plate on a stick he holds in his mouth, all while playing a theremin.

Arthur: Who knew he could play the theremin while balancing plates.

The flashback ends.

Arthur: And then there's...

He stops. Fern comes from the other side wearing a karate outfit and doing martial arts moves.

Fern: Hey-yah!

Arthur: Aah!

Fern: Hey-yah! Hey-yah!

Arthur: Fern? I didn't know you were into martial arts.

Fern: The ancient wisdom of the master says, "Even a mighty oak was once a nut like you."

She bows and leaves.

Arthur: I have no idea what that meant. Do you? Oh, well. Maybe this will explain it.

He pulls down a black screen.



Title Card: Fern Writes
Fern and her mom walk down a street.

Fern: Do you think Mr. Wu will have lots of great stories? He's from China, right?

Mrs. Walters: I don't know. I know his daughter from work, but I've never met him. It's nice of you to help him out, though.

Fern: Well, I had to volunteer to get my Junior Explorer's badge. Why not help a wise elder?

They reach the house and Mrs. Walters rings the doorbell.

Mr. Wu: It's open! Fern and Mrs. Walters come in. Mr. Wu sits in an armchair and turns off the TV. Hi. Are you the exterminator? The alligator's in the bathroom.

Fern: Alligator??

Mr. Wu walks up to them using a crutch.

Mr. Wu: I'm kidding. You must be Fern. I'm Tony Wu.

They shake hands.

Fern: Oh, er, pleased to meet you, Mr. Wu.

Mr. Wu: Do you know how to make Mexican food?

Fern: Um, no.

Mr. Wu: Me neither. Let's order in. The menus are in the kitchen, by the phone.

He sits down and turns on the TV.

Mrs. Walters: I'll pick you up later, sweetie. Bye!

She leaves.

***

Mr. Wu and Fern sit in front of the TV and watch golf.

Commentator (on TV): Jones needs this shot, to go on to the final.

Mr. Wu: Great. Now he's in the sand trap. He should have used the five iron. Or maybe a waffle iron.

Fern looks bored.

Fern: Did you used to play golf, Mr. Wu?

Mr. Wu: Oh yes! I played in eighteen different countries.

Fern looks excited.

Fern: Really?

Mr. Wu: Yep, they were all in the Elwood City, "Round the World" mini golf course.

Fern: Oh.

Mr. Wu holds out a bottle.

Mr. Wu: Could you get me some more mineral water? And help yourself to whatever's in the fridge.

The fridge contains a water bottle and a glass of pickles.

Fern: You don't have much in the fridge. Do you want me to go shopp...ing?

Mr. Wu has fallen asleep in the armchair.

***

Sue Ellen walks up to Fern in a school corridor.

Sue Ellen: Hi, Fern. Wanna come over Saturday afternoon? We're going to break in our new croquet set.

Fern: Sorry, I have to help Mr. Wu then. I'm volunteering to get my Junior Explorers Service badge

Sue Ellen: Oh, too bad. Wait... Not Tony Wu?

Fern: Yeah, why?

Sue Ellen: (gasps) I heard he lives in Elwood City but I thought it was just a rumor. Has he taught you anything?

Fern: What would he teach me?

Sue Ellen: Kung Fu. He's a grand master.

Fern: He is?

***

In Sue Ellen’s room she gets out a DVD while Fern looks at a book with newspaper cutouts about the young Tony Wu.

Sue Ellen: In the nineteen-sixties and seventies Tony Wu was one of the biggest names in martial arts. He won tons of trophies and created his own Kung Fu style, called Laughing Dragon. He even made a few films. I have one here: “Master Wu and the Rebel Monks”. It's a terrible movie, but it's really cool to see Tony Wu's moves.

She starts the DVD on her computer.

A Chinese temple in the mountains is shown. In a hall a young man sweeps the floor while a white-bearded man watches.

Student: One year I have spent with you, and you have taught me nothing! Only how to sweep and swat flies.

Master Wu: Oh, is that so?

He walks to a basket, lifts it with his foot and kicks it to the student.

Student: (gasps)

He deflects the basket with the broom. Master Wu flies at him and catches the basket in mid-air.

Master Wu: You see, any practice, even sweeping, is Kung Fu, if you learn to focus your mind.

He drops the basket on the student’s foot.

Student: (groans)

Master Wu: Always pay attention to your surroundings. Now, make soup! The student bows and leaves. Master Wu strokes his long beard which comes off. Whoops!

He puts the beard back on and does some moves.

Fern and Sue Ellen watch.

Fern: Wow! I had no idea who I was helping! You think he'll teach me some Kung Fu? I'd love to be able to do stuff like fly through the air!

Sue Ellen: He retired a while ago, but you never know.

***

Fern rings Mr. Wu’s bell.

Mr. Wu: It's open!

Fern comes in and bows.

Fern: Master Wu, I'm ready.

Mr. Wu: Me too. Golf's just about to start. Shall we order some Thai food?

Fern: Aren't there some chores you'd like me to do?

Mr. Wu: Well, I thought it could wait till after lunch, but there are one or two little things.

***

Mr. Wu shows Fern a pile of letters on his desk.

Mr. Wu: Just file it all alphabetically. Angry letters go in the trash. Thanks, Fern.

He leaves.

Fern: Filing? What kind of martial art can I learn from this?

A small Master Wu appears in the air.

Master Wu: Any practice is Kung Fu, if you learn to focus your mind. Now, file!

He disappears. Fern starts filing.

***

Fern dusts off bookshelves.

Fern: Hi-yah! (coughs)

***

Fern hangs shirts in a closet while standing on a footstool.

Fern: Hoo! Wah! Hoo! Wah! Hoo! Wah! Hoowahhh!

She falls down.

***

Tony Wu is asleep in his armchair.

Fern: Any further instructions, Master Wu?

Mr. Wu: Hh? Oh, no, you did plenty. Thanks, Fern.

Fern: Perhaps you can teach me sweeping style next week.

Mr. Wu: Sweeping style? What's that?

Fern: I see. I'm not ready. I will try harder.

She leaves.

Mr. Wu: Hm?

***

Fern walks down a school corridor wearing a mongkon.

Binky: Hey, Fern. What's with the funny ribbon?

Fern: I'm in training. I can't talk about it. I can only say, I'm learning from a master.

Binky: A master of what? Headbands?

Fern: I would advise you not to mess with me. I know filing style. She tries a fighting stance. Hiii-yah!

Binky: Um... okay. I'll let you know if I run into any papers that need filing.

***

Fern does Tony Wu’s dishes.

In her imagination she is wearing a karate gi and throws plates neatly on top of one another on the table.

Fern: Ha! Hoo-ha! Hoo-ha! Hoo-ha! Hoo-ha! Ha! She does several handsprings and slides across the kitchen on the tip of a broom. Hoo! Ha!

She bows.

The fantasy ends.

Mr. Wu: A-hem. I think you've cleaned that enough. Fern drops a plate. But now you can start gluing it.

Fern: I'm so sorry, Master Wu.

Mr. Wu: It's just a plate. I'll fix it later. Now, about all this "Master" stuff...

Fern: Well, isn't that what students call their... I mean, you're teaching me Kung Fu, right?

Mr. Wu: What? (laughs) Now that's funny! (laughs)

Fern: Wait, so all these chores are just...

Mr. Wu: Helping out an old guy with bad knees. And you've done a good job too, except for the plate.

Fern: I don't believe it. All this time, I thought I was really learning something.

Mr. Wu: Wait, I'm rusty, but if you really want to learn a martial art, I can teach you a few things.

Fern: You can? Thank you, Master!

Mr. Wu: But no calling me "Master". I'm Tony. Now get me some mineral water, grasshopper.

***

Mr. Wu and Fern face each other in the living room.

Mr. Wu: Today, we're gonna try some Tai Chi. That means, Supreme Ultimate Fist. Do what I do. First posture, stand quietly.

Fern: Just stand?

Mr. Wu: Just stand. A correct stance is very important. Okay, now comes Horse stance.

He bends his legs.

Fern: So, when do I learn to climb walls and walk on trees and stuff?

Mr. Wu: That's just in the movies. Now get ready for the next stance, bow and sit.

***

Sue Ellen walks along a school corridor with Fern.

Sue Ellen: I can't believe you're studying Tai Chi with Mr. Wu. You're so lucky!

Fern: But it's so slow. I'm sorry, but I don't think I could defeat an enemy with bow and sit stance.

She demonstrates the stance.

Binky: What are you training for, Fern? The slow motion Olympics? (laughs)

***

Mr. Wu and Fern have another practice session in the living room.

Mr. Wu: Ding bell on toe. Step left foot out. Toe to ground. Keep back straight.

Fern: Tony? Isn't martial arts about fighting opponents?

Mr. Wu: Tai Chi is different. It's a soft style. About focus and balance.

Fern: But you studied Kung Fu and combat style when you were young in China, right?

Mr. Wu: China? I grew up in California. And I learned Kung Fu from my Aunt Shirley.

Fern: You're not even from China?!

Mr. Wu: You'll have to let go of your ideas of how you thought things were, and go with the flow.

Fern: Is that an ancient proverb?

Mr. Wu: (laughs) It's from the sixties. I learned it in Berkeley. So, shall we continue?

Fern: Why don't we go out to the park and do our lesson there?

Mr. Wu: The park? Oh, er... no!

Fern: Come on, I've never seen you out of the house the whole time I've been coming here. It'll do you good.

Mr. Wu: Er...it's too far.

Fern: Too far? This from a Kung Fu master?

Mr. Wu: I...don't have the right shoes.

Fern: (giggles) Maybe you'll see some friends.

Mr. Wu: Friends? You heard my jokes, Fern. I-I-I don't have any friends.

Fern: (laughs)

She hands him his walking aid.

Mr. Wu: I'll go, but I'm not gonna like it.

***

Mr. Wu and Fern practice Tai Chi in the park.

Lady: Mind if I join in?

Mr. Wu: Please. You know, Fern...I think I like this after all.

Two more ladies join them.

Fern: Sometimes you have to let go of your expectations and...

Fern+Mr. Wu: Go with the flow!
