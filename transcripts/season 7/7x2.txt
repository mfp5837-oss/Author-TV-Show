Introduction
Someone watches from the bushes as Mrs. Read hang up laundry in the yard.

Mrs. Read: (hums)

When she goes back into the house, the someone steps forward and spots a toy mouse.

Mrs. Read takes socks out of the washing machine. She hears a noise. The toy mouse is thrown across the room.

Mrs. Read: D.W.? Is that you?

She walks upstairs while the someone approaches the socks.

Title Card: Pal Searches
Mrs. Read puts away laundry in D.W.’s room while D.W. sits on the bed wearing only one sock.

D.W.: How can I wear my red-and-purple dress with only one red and purple sock?

Mrs. Read: I'm sorry, honey, but I can't find your other sock. Are you sure you put it in the hamper?

D.W.: I bet Arthur stole it!

Mrs. Read: D.W., why on Earth would Arthur want to steal your socks?

Pal: I don't know! Maybe he wanted to use them as ear-warmers for his crazy dog!

She notices Pal beside Kate’s playpen.

Pal: (barks)

D.W.: Hey! Get away from Kate! You'll give her germs!

Pal: (whines)

Mrs. Read: D.W., relax. They're just playing.

Pal: She ruined my concentration. I was so close to making a hole large enough for you to escape.

Kate: That's all right. We'll try again later. She looks at Mrs. Read consoling D.W. D.W. seems very upset about that sock on her foot.

Pal: I'll say! Don't you people usually put two of those things on your feet? Why does she only have one?

Kate: I don't know.

D.W.: But then how else could it have disappeared? I need that sock! It's my special sock and my foot's cold!

Kate: She's really upset now! This sock thing's worse than I thought!

Pal: (yawns) Well, I'd better go find it. If I don't, she could be like this for days!

***

Pal goes into the basement and looks behind the washing machine. Then he looks inside the washing machine.

Pal: No...just lint. He eats some. Good lint. (chuckles) Get a hold of yourself, boy! You have a job to do!

Pal sniffs around and finds a brown hair.

Pal: Hello! What's this? He sees a fish scale. And this?

He continues sniffing.

***

Mr. and Mrs. Read move the drying machine and look behind it. Pal shows the hair to Kate.

Pal: See? A hair. And it doesn't belong to any of us, I can tell you that much.

Kate: (gasps) You think...?

Pal: Yes, an intruder! Look, I smelled the sock here. He must have dropped it while he was escaping and he likes fish. He spits a fish scale into Kate’s hand. It's a scale. I find them sometimes in my Seafood Supreme.

Kate: Excellent work, Pal! Now, who do we know who is cunning, devious, and likes fish?

Pal+Kate: Nemo!

***

Pal is on a leash and pulls Arthur towards the Frenskys’ apartment building.

Arthur: Easy, Pal! Slow down!

Francine opens the door and finds Arthur and Pal outside.

Arthur: Hi, Francine! Pal pulled me here. What you doing?

While the kids talk, Nemo walks around Francine’s legs.

Nemo: Well, well, well! If it isn't Arthur's runt! I wish I could say look what the cat dragged in, but, alas, I didn't have the pleasure of dragging you!

Pal: Hand it over, sugar puss.

Nemo: Hand what over?

Pal starts chasing Nemo.

Pal: (barks)

Nemo: (meows)

Pal corners Nemo in the kitchen.

Pal: Give me back that sock you stole.

Nemo: But I didn't steal any sock.

Pal: Then how do you explain this?

He coughs up a brown hair.

Nemo: Ew, what is it?

Pal: It's your hair. I found it in our laundry room.

Nemo: That's not my hair. It's brown. My fur is black and white. Are you colorblind?

Pal: As a matter of fact, I am.

Nemo: I was never in your house, dog! Ask that silly toad who hangs around your yard if you don't believe me!

Francine picks up Nemo.

Francine: Bloo-blee bloo-blah-bloo blee-blah, mooshka.

Arthur picks up Pal’s leash.

Pal: But, if it wasn't you, who did steal the sock?

Nemo: Beats me! But remember that ham bone you buried in the flower patch last spring? It was delicious!

Pal: (gasps, growls)

Arthur: Easy, Pal! Slow down!

He pulls Pal away.

***

Mrs. Read lies in a deckchair in the yard. Kate talks to Toadie.

Toadie: Since I left D.W., things have been good. I have my own pad nearby.

Kate: Do you ever get lonely living on your own?

Toadie: It would be nice to meet some other amphibians, but I just hate the pond scene.

Kate: Toadie, have you seen any strange animals going into the laundry room?

Toadie: Now that you mention it... something did go in there just yesterday.

Pal joins them with muddy paws.

Pal: Well, my ham bone is definitely gone. I dug up the daisies, the roses... Nothing!

Kate: Pal, Toadie saw something.

Pal: Was it a cat?

Toadie: I'm not sure what it was, but it was much larger than a cat and it was breathing heavily, like this... (breathes loudly)

Pal: What if it's a raccoon? Or a Tibble?

Kate: Whatever it is, it has to be stopped! And I think I know just how to do it.

***

That night, Pal gets the baby monitor from Mrs. Read’s nightstand.

Kate (on phone): Pal, do you read me? Are you in position?

Pal: I'm coming! I'm coming!

He pushes a plate with donuts and the baby phone through the kitchen and out through the dog flap.

Pal: What's a stakeout without doughnuts?

From D.W.’s room, Kate watches Pal drag the donuts behind a tree. She is holding the other baby monitor.

Kate: All right, Pal. Now we wait.

***

Time passes. The moon moves across the sky. Pal sleeps on his back beside an empty plate. Kate sees a dark shadow moving towards a sock in the grass.

Kate: Pal! He's here! The suspect is approaching the sock!

Pal gets up and runs to the shadow.

Pal: Aha! Caught you red... (gasps) It can't be!

Amigo holds a sock in his mouth.

Kate: And to think his name means friend!

Amigo: It's not what it looks like! I mean, sí, I'm stealing your socks, but it's for a good cause!

Pal: You mean, there's a charity for humans without socks? Even so, Amigo...

Amigo: No, it's more important than that! But it is difficult to explain. Come, I must show you.

Kate (on phone): Be careful, Pal. If you're not back by sunrise, I'll call the parents.

Pal nods in the direction of the window and follows Amigo.

***

Amigo and Pal walk along a street.

Amigo: Every pet knows that humans cannot survive without us. Well, a long time ago, some pets decided to form a secret group called the Furmasons. The Furmasons were dedicated to finding new ways of helping people. They trained dogs to lead people who are blind and cats to catch mice.

Amigo and Pal reach a factory yard and walk towards a door.

Amigo: They even sent some of their members to space, but their greatest gift for the humans was this... He knocks on the door. A slit opens and two eyes look out. The door is opened. ...the sock exchange!

Pal walks into a large room full of animals (cats, dogs, reptiles, etc.) all carrying socks.

Scottish Terrier: I've got an argyle here. Who needs an argyle?

Pal: This is amazing. But how does all this help people?

Amigo: It's very complicated. Ah, here's someone who can explain it to you. A spaniel wearing glasses and a tie walks by. Good evening, Mr. Greenspaniel. My friend here wants to know what the purpose of the sock market is.

Mr. Greenspaniel: Our challenge is to ensure that the extending favorable macroeconomic performance bolster the capabilities of all to share in the prosperity that is being generated.

He walks away.

Pal: I didn't understand a word he said.

Amigo: Actually, no one does. All I know is we take their socks, they buy more. It's good for the economy.

Pal: So that's why there are so many missing socks!

The walk over to where a cat is holding up a sock for other animals to inspect.

Cat: From the hairy...

Pal: But, Amigo, the one you took was very important to D.W. I'm going to have to ask for it back.

Amigo: Sorry, Pal, I don't have it. It's out there somewhere. The only way to get it back is to try to trade it for another sock.

Pal: You mean I should... (gulps) ...play the sock market?

Amigo: It's easy. Just remember - always buy low and sell high.

***

Kate drops a baby booty out of her crib and Pal takes it away.

At the sock market, Pal offers the booty and is given a shabby sock in return.

While Mrs. Read is doing the laundry, Kate takes out several socks that Pal takes away.

When Pal arrives at the sock market, other animals snatch away the socks. A piece of lint falls down which Mr. Greenspaniel takes away.

Pal sits beside Kate’s crib.

Pal: I've lost it all, Kate! Every last booty! We're ruined!

Kate: It's okay, Pal. I never liked wearing them anyway! I'm only sorry we never got D.W.'s sock back.

D.W. sits on her bed and plays with a sock puppet.

Pal: Look, Kate. She misses it so much, she's pretending that the other one is alive.

D.W.: Blah-blee-blah-blah...

Kate+Pal: (sigh)

Amigo calls from outside the window.

Amigo: Pal! Kate! Good news! I found the investor who has your sock! I told him you were all out of bootees, but he said he might trade the sock for something else. He'll meet you in your yard tomorrow.

Kate: Thank you, Amigo. to Pal: We have to gather up all the lost things you can find.

Pal runs off.

***

The next day, Mr. Read is planting flowers in the yard. Kate sits beside him when Pal calls her.

Pal: Kate!

Kate follows Pal to a blanket on which several items are lying.

Kate: Very tastefully arranged, Pal! I'm sure there's something here that he'll want.

Pal: Look! That must be him.

A dachshund holding D.W’s sock comes around the corner. A toad sits on its back holding a sausage in his mouth like a cigar. He jumps off.

Mr. Toad: Come on, Hans, can't you move any faster? Wait for me here!

Hans: (whines)

Mr. Toad: Mr. Toad's the name, making deals is my game, haha. So, you're the pup who wants his sock back, huh?

Pal: Is that a... (gulps) ...hot dog in your mouth, Mr. Toad?

Mr. Toad: 100% pure beef, kid! A cat in the deli gets them for me cheap. So, down to business. What do you got for the sock?

Kate: How about this? I think it's called "money."

She holds up a five dollar bill.

Mr. Toad: Nah, found a ton of that stuff at the Crosswire place. Used it to wallpaper my pad.

Kate: What about this nice shiny set of keys?

She holds up a set of keys.

Mr. Toad: Is it to a motorcar?

Kate: Um. I'm not sure. What's a "motorcar"?

Mr. Toad: Nah, I'm not supposed to drive anyway.

Kate: How about a Woogle?

Mr. Toad: Ta! Please! That fad went out with the nineties.

Kate: All right. What about this almost new delicious cherry lolli... She notices that she is holding an empty lollipop stick. Pal!

Pal spits out the lollipop end.

Pal: A-huh.

Mr. Toad: Slim pickings, folks! You'll have to do a lot better than that! However, there is one thing here I could use... He jumps on Pal’s head. Him! Yes, you! You see, my current dog is getting a little old. Time to trade up!

Kate: You can't have Pal! He's my best friend!

She picks Mr. Toad off Pal.

Mr. Toad: Come on, baby! He's not even your species! What's it gonna be, the mangy dog or the beautiful sock? Kate hugs Pal. The dog or the sock? The dog or the sock?

Toadie hops out of the bushes.

Toadie: Mr. Toad? Is it really you?

Mr. Toad: Toadie! I-I haven't seen you since you were a tadpole!

Toadie: Oh, Mr. Toad! I've missed you so much!

They rub their heads together.

Mr. Toad: I've missed you too, Toadie! Are you free for a slug or two?

Toadie: Sure! They hop off together followed by Hans. You know, I recognized that croak right away. I said to myself, "Toady, that is your old friend Mr. Toad." And, sure enough...

Kate picks up the sock that Mr. Toad left lying on the blanket.

Kate: Well, we did it! I hope D.W. will be happy! Who would have thought a sock could cause so much trouble?

Pal: I'll say! Nasty things! Can't understand why any creature would wear them! Kate, you wouldn't have traded me, would you?

Kate: Not for all the socks in the world, Pal! Pal is about to eat Mr. Toad’s hotdog. Oh, Pal! You're not going to put that thing in your mouth, are you?

Pal: If there's one thing I’ve learned from my days at the sock market, it's this: it's a dog-eat-dog world!

He eats the hotdog.
