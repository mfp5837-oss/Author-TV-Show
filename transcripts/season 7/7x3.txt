Introduction
Buster looks at the ice cream in the Powers’ ice cream shop. Arthur is already licking his cone.

Arthur: Buster, would you make up your mind already!

Buster: Just a second. Okay, a scoop of tattoo goo, a scoop of calamari crunch, a scoop of pretzels and cream, and a scoop of dulce de leche on a sugar cone with jalapeño jimmies.

Arthur: Have you ever tried to bite off more than you can chew? Buster certainly has. Brain hands Buster his huge ice-cream cone. How on Earth are you going to eat all that?

Buster: First, I lick to south from north. Mmmm. Calamari. Next, I lick from east to west.

Arthur: Buster look out!

Buster's ice cream falls on the ground.

Buster: (gasps) Ice cream down! Quick, I need the paddles! Brain gives Buster two ice cream paddles. Stand back! I need room!

Holding the cone with his teeth, he scoops up the ice cream with the paddles.

Buster: This has to be a perfect shot... and... clear!

He throws up the ice cream. It lands on his face. Brain claps.

Arthur: You really should have been a performing seal.

Buster: Heyyyy... looks like I picked up some extra nuts.

Arthur: Ugh!

Title Card: Francine Bangs Gong
Francine walks into the school cafeteria with her tray.

Arthur: Francine!

Buster: Over here!

Brain: Sit here!

Francine: Hi guys.

She sits with the boys.

Arthur: So, how do you feel?

Buster: Have you been sleeping well?! A-any shortness of breath? Is your tongue coated?

He tries to look into Francine’s mouth.

Francine: Buster, I'm fine! What's with you guys?

Brain: We just want you to be ready for the bowling tournament on Saturday.

Francine: Relax. I've been bowling with my dad since I could walk. We're gonna roll right over 'em.

Arthur: Boy, won't it be nice to beat Mighty Mountain for a change?

Buster: I'll say. Remember when you two were having that really big fight and Mighty Mountain creamed us at soccer?

Flashback from "The Big Blow-Up": Brain and Francine score the winning goal.

Lakewood Players: (cheer)

They lift up Francine and Brain.

The flashback ends.

Brain: As I recall, we won that game.

Buster: Oh yeah. Well... what about that time it was Friday the 13th and you were really superstitious? Mighty Mountain clobbered us at softball.

Flashback from "Friday the 13th": Brain hits a home run.

Arthur: We won that game too.

Buster: Oh... yeah... but what about the Jai Alai tournament against them?

All: What a disaster.

Arthur: But that won't happen this time. 'Cause we have the best bowler in Elwood City on our side.

Muffy walks past them with her tray.

Muffy: Did someone say bowling? Can I play? Those two-toned shoes are so fabulously retro chic.

Brain: Sorry, Muffy, the tournament regulations state that a team can only have four players.

Muffy: Ohhh... and I was going to get daddy to have some really cool bowling shirts made....

Arthur: Umm...

Francine: Maybe you could be our, umm.. bowling fashion consultant.

Muffy: Goody! But we're going to have to do something about your hair.

Francine looks insulted.

***

Francine stands in her room in her pajamas and practices bowling. She is using Polly Locket dolls as pins.

Francine: It all comes down to this... Frensky has to bowl a strike... the crowd is breathless...

Her mom comes in with the laundry. Francine hides the ball behind her back.

Mrs. Frensky: Francine, what did I tell you about bowling inside the house?

Francine: Sorry, mom, just psyching up for the big tournament Saturday.

Mrs. Frensky: You can't go bowling Saturday. It's your cousin Seth's bar mitzvah.

Francine: What?! But I can't miss the biggest tournament ever for some lame bar mitzvah!

Mrs. Frensky: It's an important family event. Besides, it'll be fun. There'll be lots of food and dancing, and you'll get to see all your cousins.

She walks out.

Francine: But mom! She drops the ball. The whole school's counting on me! Pleaaase? And nobody'll even notice I'm not there! You've gotta let me go!

***

Arthur, Buster, Brain, and Muffy stand around Francine at her locker.

Arthur: What do you mean you can't bowl on Saturday?

Buster: She's sick! I knew it! Her tongue was furry!

Francine: No Buster. It's my cousin's bar mitzvah.

Muffy: Bar mitzvah? What's that?

Brain: It's the Jewish celebration of adulthood. For males it's called a bar mitzvah, and for females it's called a bat mitzvah.

Francine: Whatever. All I know is you turn thirteen, you go to temple, recite some prayers, and everyone says you're an adult. Then there's a boring party with lots of presents.

Brain: It's more significant than that, Francine. To truly understand you have to go back to the beginning of the Jewish people. When Abraham and Sarah begat Isaac, who begat Jacob, who begat Joseph, who begat...

Arthur and Buster exchange looks.

Arthur: The point is, without Francine we're gonna get creamed again.

Francine: Guys, I'm sorry. But what am I supposed to do? I can't be in two places at the same time.

Brain: Maybe you can...

***

The kids sit in the treehouse where Brain shows a self-drawn map of Elwood City.

Brain: According to my calculations, it is possible to attend your cousin's bar mitzvah and bowl for Lakewood. But it will require precision timing.

A fantasy shows Francine putting on a dress.

Brain (narrator): Step one: wear your party dress over your bowling shirt and pants.

Seth recites a prayer in the synagogue.

Brain (narrator): Step two: Seth becomes an adult no later than 12:30 PM.

Francine drinks a glass of punch and checks her watch.

Brain (narrator): Step three: Drink one glass of punch at the party. Then, at precisely 12:52 PM sneak out the back door.

Francine sneaks out and gets on her bike.

Brain (narrator): Step four: Ride your bike over to the...

Francine (offscreen): Wait! The bike disappears. How does my bike get there?

Arthur drops Francine’s bike off in front of the building. He is wearing his bowling clothes.

Brain (narrator): On his way to the bowling alley, Arthur will leave it there for you.

Francine (offscreen): Don't forget my bowling shoes.

Arthur puts the bike helmet in a basket on the bike.

Arthur (offscreen): Wait! How do I get to the tournament?

Brain (narrator): The team fashion consultant will pick you up.

Arthur gets into the Crosswire limousine.

Brain (narrator): Now, where was I?

Buster (offscreen): You're just at the part when twin jets come out of the back of Francine's bike and she rockets up to...

Francine’s bike flies into the air and freezes.

All (offscreen): Buster!

Francine’s bike drops to the ground. Francine rides on.

Brain (narrator): Step five: You beat Mighty Mountain for us.

Francine bowls a strike. She receives a trophy and celebrates with her team.

Kids: (cheer)

The fantasy ends. Brain has drawn many red arrows on the map.

Brain: Final step: Return to the party before anyone misses you. So, what do you think?

Francine: I like it.

***

On Saturday, Francine puts on her dress over her bowling clothes. The buttons keep coming undone.

Mrs. Frensky (outside): Hurry up Francine! We're gonna be late.

Francine: Almost ready!

She is about to put on pants when her mom comes in. She drops the pants.

Mrs. Frensky: C'mon, let's go.

***

At the synagogue, Seth stands on the bimah and reads from the Torah.

Seth: (chants in Hebrew)

The Frenskys sit in the audience.

Francine: I didn't know Seth could speak Hebrew.

Mrs. Frensky: He can't really. But he studied this passage for a year to be ready for today.

Francine: Wow.

***

The Frenskys walk out of the synagogue where people are congratulating Seth. Francine checks her digital watch, it is 12:52.

Francine: C'mon dad, we gotta get to the party!

She pulls him away.

Mr. Frensky: What's the rush?

Francine: I'm... starving. Yeah. Starving.

***

The Frenskys walk into the party room. A live band is playing and people are dancing.

Francine: This is so cool!

Mr. Frensky: Let's see you get your groove on, Frankie. Come with me and dance.

Francine’s watch says 1:05.

Francine: Umm... maybe later. Right now I have to go to... er, to the bathroom.

Francine runs out of the back entrance where her bike is leaning against a dumpster. She puts on the helmet and checks the bike’s basket. It is empty.

Francine: (gasps) Arthur!

***

Francine rides her bike. Her dress gets stuck in the chain and she gets off to get it loose.

Francine: C'mon... c'mon...

She now has oil stains on her dress.

***

At the bowling alley, a burly Mighty Mountain player bowls a strike.

MM Team: (cheer)

The Lakewood players look uncomfortable. Francine joins them and confronts Arthur.

Francine: Where are my bowling shoes?!

Arthur: Oops. Umm... at my house? Hey, at least I remembered your bike.

Brain: There's no time to argue. You're twelve minutes and eighteen seconds late. Just go rent a pair.

Francine runs to the shoe rental.

Francine: Quick! I need a pair of shoes... size four.

Attendant: Sorry, all I got is a ten.

Francine: Aw...

Francine walks back to the others in oversized shoes. She is also wearing her dress under her bowling shirt, since she left her pants at home.

Francine: I'm coming, I'm coming!

MM Team: (chuckle)

Muffy hands Francine a ball with a picture of a flamingo.

Francine: What's this?! You forgot my bowling ball too?

Muffy: No, but as the team's fashion consultant I thought your ball looked a little shabby. So I got you a brand new one.

Francine: This... this looks like a toy. And my fingers don't fit!

Muffy: Well excuse me! Nobody's perfect. You think I'm happy with your ridiculous combo?

Brain hands Francine a ball.

Brain: Here, use mine. Just bowl!

Francine gives them dirty looks and walks up to the lane. She bowls. So does a Mighty Mountain player. The Lakewood kids are gathered around a monitor checking the score.

Brain: Hm, still thirty-five points behind.

Francine: It's these shoes! My feet keep slipping out of them!

Buster: Maybe we can tape them to your feet. I'll find some tape. I've got some time before my next turn.

Francine: Time? Oh no! I'm late! I've gotta go! She slips out of her shoes and runs off. Someone bowl for me! I'll be back!

***

At the party, waiters are just taking away the empty plates when Francine returns.

Mrs. Frensky: Francine, where've you been? I told your waiter to hold your food, but...

Francine: I went outside to get some fresh air. I was feeling kinda queasy.

Her dad feels her forehead.

Mr. Frensky: Hm, you are a little clammy.

Francine: So, did I miss the overcooked peas and underdone chicken? A waiter puts a plate in front of her. Whoa! We're having roast beef? This looks delicious!

Her mom takes the plate away.

Mrs. Frensky: Sorry Francine, but if you have an upset stomach, I don't think you should be eating meat. to waiter: Could you just bring us a bowl of some plain broth?

Francine: Aww....

Her stomach rumbles.

***

Francine returns to the bowling alley.

Francine: Okay, I'm here! The others are looking horrified at Muffy who is about to bowl with both hands. What's she doing??

Arthur: She had to take your turn because you were late.

Francine: But... she's gonna lose us the tournament! That's the worst bowling stance I've ever seen.

Buster: Actually, she's kinda been getting the hang of it. Oh, here's the tape for your shoes.

Francine takes the tape and watches Muffy.

Muffy: (grunts)

She throws the ball instead of rolling it. While it is rolling towards the last standing pin, Francine checks her watch (1:59).

Francine: Arrgh! This is taking forever! Let me take over.

Brain: You can't, Francine. The rules say she has to finish her frame. Why don't you go back to the party and return for the final round?

***

When Francine returns to the party, people are just finishing a dance and clap for the band.

Francine: Oh no! Did I miss the Horah?

Mrs. Frensky: Nope, you're just in time.

Everybody joins hands and dances in a circle to the tune of "Hava Nagila". In the middle of the circle, Seth’s family lift him up on a chair. Francine's dad notices the oil on her dress.

Mr. Frensky: What happened to your dress?

Francine: Oh, um, heh. A waiter spilled roast beef on me. What a klutz. Hey. Do I get to go on the chair too? I'm feeling much better.

Mr. Frensky: Sure, after Seth and his parents get their ride.

Seth’s grandmother is lifted on the chair.

Mr. Frensky: Okay Frankie, your turn.

Francine notices that the clock says 2:46.

Francine: Uh, shouldn't Catherine go first? She's older. Besides, I need to get some water. To clean my dress.

She runs off. The parents exchange looks.

***

At the bowling alley, it is Muffy’s turn to bowl. Francine tapes her bowling shoes to her feet.

Francine: There! Mighty Mountain, look out!

Muffy’s ball rolls slowly and manages to knock over the last two pins.

Brain: I-I don’'t believe it.. she got a 7/10 split!

Arthur: Yes!

Arthur, Buster and Brain lift Muffy up.

Arthur+Buster+Brain: (cheer)

Francine: You.. you mean it's over?

She looks disappointed and guilty.

Arthur+Buster+Brain: Muffy! Muffy! Muffy!

Muffy: I chipped a nail.

***

At the party, people are getting ready to go. Francine joins her family.

Mrs. Frensky: So, where were you this time?

Francine: Uhh... at the punch bowl?

Mr. Frensky: You know, I thought it was your nose that grew when you told lies. But, apparently, it's your bowling shoes.

Francine realizes she is still wearing her oversized bowling shoes.

Francine: (gasps)

***

The next day, Arthur and Brain watch Francine wash the side of a garbage truck outside the Frenskys’ apartment block.

Francine: After this, I have to mop the kitchen floor and then wash out Nemo's litterbox.

Brain: Harsh.

Arthur: I guess your mom and dad were really upset you missed part of Seth's bar mitzvah.

Francine: Yeah, but so am I. I missed a really great party for that stupid bowling tournament.

Brain: Cheer up, Francine. There's another party coming up.

Francine: There is? Whose?

Brain: Yours. If you start preparing now, you'll be ready for your bat mitzvah in five years, ninety-three days, and two-hundred and seventy minutes and forty-eight seconds... forty-seven seconds... forty-six seconds... forty-five sec...

He looks at his watch. Francine throws her sponge in his face.
