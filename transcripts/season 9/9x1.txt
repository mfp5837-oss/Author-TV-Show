Introduction
Zutzut, Waggy and the Commander stand in front of a gorge with a river.

Zutzut: This map doesn't show a river!

Commander: Treacherous topographers! Trying to throw us off the trail with fictitious flimflam!

Zutzut throws a rope with a grappling hook around a tree on the other side.

Zutzut: Well, there's no getting around it. We must cross, eh, Waggy?

Waggy: (barks)

Zutzut and Waggy start walking the rope. Suddenly it snaps.

The image turns into a comic book which Fern is reading in bed.

Mrs. Walters: Lights out, Fern!

Fern: I'm in the middle of “Zutzut and the Temple of The Condor”.

Her mother looks in.

Mrs. Walters: You need your sleep, sweetie. Tomorrow you're climbing a mountain just like Zutzut.

Fern: You're right. Tomorrow it's gonna be my adventure. In her imagination Zutzut on the comic’s cover turns into herself. “Fernfern and the Secret of Moose Mountain”.

Title Card: Balloon
Prunella, Fern, Muffy and Jenna stand in the Moose Mountain Ranger Center.

Ranger Jackie: Welcome to Moose Mountain, everybody. My name's Ranger Jackie and I'd be happy to answer all your questions.

Jenna: Any chance of seeing a pied-billed grebe on the trip?

Ranger Jackie: We’ve got a bird watcher! Here's a pamphlet of all the species you might see on the mountain.

Prunella: What about interesting rocks, like yellow quartz or crystal?

Ranger Jackie: A budding geologist! Here's something on Moose Mountain's interesting rock formations.

Muffy: What about shopping?

Ranger Jackie: We have a great gift shop right here! Check out these hats!

She puts on a cap with antlers.

Muffy: I think it's time for you to expand your retail horizons. You should carry one of these. It's a global positioning system, the latest in navigation technology. I got it at the Sherpa Image. Our position is thirty-nine steps, forty degrees North by Northwest from our minivan. Whatever. Now I'll ask it to give me the location of the nearest mall. The GPS beeps. Twenty-seven miles away?? We’ve fallen off the face of the earth.

Fern: I have a question. What can you tell me about the dark side of the mountain?

Ranger Jackie: What?

Fern: You know, a little history, something like an abandoned goldmine haunted by the wails of those who died there driven mad by their greedy obsessions. Or maybe a ravine with the remains of a wagon that plunged over the side, the bones and dreams of the pioneers crushed and shattered on the pitiless rocks below. Or how about a desolate campsite where explorers shared their last rations before disappearing forever into the harsh and silent wilderness.

Ranger Jackie: Mercy, no!

Francine joins them.

Francine: Hi, Ranger Jackie. Don't worry, I've already got a map.

Ranger Jackie: Hiya, Frankie. Francine's dad used to bring her to our “Pee-wees at the Peak”-program. What route are you taking?

Francine: We're going north on the blue trail straight up.

Muffy: Is there a place where the top of the mountain is closer to the bottom?

Francine: No.

Muffy: But that looks like a lot of hiking. How long is this going to take us, exactly?

Francine: It usually takes me and my dad three hours, but today I intend to set a new Frensky record.

Mr. Frensky comes in.

Mr. Frensky: All right, happy hikers, before we hit the trail. I'm going to assign each of you a hiking buddy.

Francine: Dad! I don't need a buddy! I could do this trail with my eyes closed!

Mr. Frensky: That's why you're going to be Fern's buddy. This is her first big hike and you're an old pro. Francine and Fern look less than pleased. Prunella and Jenna, you two are buddies, and, Muffy, I'll be your buddy.

Muffy: Fine, but as my buddy you should know that I'm not contractually obligated to complete this hike and reserve the right to exercise the bail-out clause at any time.

Mr. Frensky: Um, okay. Remember, everyone, stick with your buddy and look out for each other on the trail.

Francine: (to Fern): Keep up or get lost, get the picture?!

She sets a stopwatch and runs off.

Mr. Frensky: Francine, pace yourself, kiddo!

***

Fern ties her shoe on the trail.

Francine: Just double knot it! We're losing time.

Fern imagines herself as Zutzut and Francine as the Commander.

Francine: Move it, Fernfern, you sloth-footed slowpoke!

Waggy: (barks)

Fern runs after Francine who is standing at a gorge.

Fern: Commander, may I remind you that one wrong move could trigger a rockslide.

Francine: Plotting, perambulating pedestrian!

She stamps her foot. A nearby mountain top collapses and rocks roll down at them. They run away.

Francine+Fern: Ahhhh!

The fantasy ends.

Mr. Frensky: Francine, stop! We're all back at the rest area. It's time for lunch.

***

The group have lunch at a wooden building with bathrooms.

Muffy: If I ordered a pizza from here, we'd have to wait three hours and forty-two minutes for delivery, and that's assuming good traffic conditions.

A squirrel sits on a rock.

Prunella: Hey, everybody, look!

Muffy: It's so cute.

Jenna: Shh! Don't scare him. This'll be a great picture!

Muffy: Hey, little guy!

A gust of wind blows Francine’s map away. Fern runs after it.

Fern: (gasps) The map!

Francine: Okay, lunch is over. Let's hit the trail!

Fern: But we have to find the...

Francine: Fern, I already told you, I know the way. Let's move! Now!

She puts on her backpack and leaves. The others are still watching the squirrel. Fern runs after Francine.

***

Francine and Fern reach a rope bridge.

Fern: A rope bridge! Zutzut crossed one of these in "The Pearl Palace".

Francine: Come on, then.

Fern: Um... Francine, I have to go to the bathroom.

Francine: Aw. Fine! Go into the bushes over there and make it quick!

Fern: No way! I'm going back to the rest area.

Francine: You're on your own then. I'm fifteen minutes ahead of schedule and I'm not going back.

She steps onto the bridge.

Mr. Frensky and Muffy are a little bit behind. Muffy is talking on her cell phone.

Muffy: Can you still hear me, Daddy? Great! How about now? Great! And now?

They reach Fern.

Mr. Frensky: Hey, roadrunners! Everything okay?

Francine: Fern has to go to the bathroom. Indoors!

Mr. Frensky: Okay. Well, we're not that far from the rest area. You two can go back and we'll all wait for you here.

The other girls are catching up.

Francine: She can go by herself!

Mr. Frensky: That's not how it works. Fern is your buddy.

Fern: Either way, I really need to go. Now!

Mr. Frensky: Francine Alice Frensky!

Francine comes back.

***

Fern comes out of the bathroom at the rest area. Francine checks her watch.

Francine: We've lost 11 minutes and 37 seconds. I know a short cut that'll get us back to the bridge faster. Come on!

They run.

***

The girls run through the woods a while. Then Francine is unsure about the way.

Fern: Francine, make up your mind.

Francine: Quiet! I'm thinking.

Fern: Are we lost?

Francine: No, I'm just not exactly sure where we are.

Fern: Yay, we’re lost! Now we can have a real adventure!

Francine checks her backpack.

Francine: Oh no, the map! How will we know which way to go?

Fern: Relax, this kind of thing happens to Zutzut all the time. Ah! A stream. Perfect. They walk along the stream. In “The Jade Crocodile”, Zutzut uses a stream to find his way to a lost subterranean city of gold.

Francine: So? We're not looking for a lost subterranean city of gold.

Fern: No, but water flows down, right? If we follow the stream against the current, it will take us to the top of the mountain. This is going to be great!

***

Fern and Francine walk on.

Fern: And then in “The Sapphire Goblet”, Zutzut gets marooned on an island of man-eating tortoises.

Francine: Where did the stream go?

The path disappears into a flooded area with dead trees.

Fern: A swamp! This is just like “Zutzut and the Cajun Queen”. We'll find some wood, build a catapult and fling ourselves across.

Francine: We can't just build a catapult. And we don't even know what's on the other side of this swamp!

Fern: But when Zutzut did it, he...

Francine: Who cares what Zutzut did?! Zutzut isn't even a real person! Why did I listen to you? This was a stupid idea!

Fern: Well, taking that short cut was even more stupid!

Francine: Why couldn't you just go to the bathroom in the bushes? I bet Zutzut does it all the time!

Fern: He does not!

Francine: I'm getting out of here!

Fern: I'm crossing the swamp!

Francine: See if I care! Francine turns and leaves. She stumbles over a branch and falls. Agh! (gasps)

Fern wades through the swamp.

Fern: (grunts)

Francine: Fern!

Fern realizes that she is stuck.

Fern: Francine! I'm stuck!

Francine: Hold on!

Francine climbs on a log in the swamp and holds out a branch to Fern. With her help Fern manages to get on dry land.

Fern: (grunts) My shoe!

The shoe sinks into the swamp. The girls sit down.

Francine: Whatever happens, we have to stick together.

Fern: I guess I'm no Zutzut.

Francine: No, but you're pretty good at figuring things out when you're just being Fern. If we could figure out which way is north, I think that's the direction that should take us to the top of the mountain.

Fern: In “Zutzut And The Jinxed Jungle”...

Francine: (sighs)

She rolls her eyes.

Fern: Just listen. He figures out north by looking at the moss on the trees. There should be more moss on the...

Fern+Francine: Northern side!

Francine: Yes! I learned that from my dad.

***

Francine helps Fern along who is hopping on one foot. / They cross a stream by crawling over a fallen tree trunk. / They climb a steep bank.

Fern: We should be getting close to the top by now.

They rest against rocks.

Francine: I give up! I think we should just scream for help. Maybe someone will hear us.

Fern+Francine: Aaaah!!

A hawk flies up.

Fern: It's a vulture! We must have “Early Bird Special” written all over us! Someday they'll find us, just two little piles of bones.

Francine: Wait a minute.

Fern: And forever after people will point to Moose Mountain and talk about the bones of the lost girls...

Francine: Fern! That's a hawk up there! A hawk! My dad and I always see them when we get close to the top of the mountain. Come on!

***

They continue and get a nice view over a valley.

Fern: It's beautiful up here!

Francine: Hey, two hours and fifty-nine minutes. I beat my record!

Fern picks up a shiny yellow rock.

Fern: Pretty rock.

Francine: And here's a hawk feather. She picks it up. Jenna would...

Fern: The others!

Ranger Jackie comes running.

Ranger Jackie: Francine! Fern!

Francine: We are in so much trouble.

***

Mr. Frensky is waiting by the car at the ranger station when Ranger Jackie brings Fern and Francine. Francine hugs her dad.

Mr. Frensky: Francine! I was so worried about you. Thank goodness you're safe. But there are going to be serious consequences for this, young lady!

Francine: I know. I'm sorry, Dad.

The girls get in the car where the others are waiting.

Prunella: Thanks for ruining our hike!

Jenna: Was it nice at the top? Because we wouldn't know!

Fern hands Prunella the shiny rock.

Fern: I found this up there. I don't know what it is, but you can have it.

Prunella: Whoa, this is yellow quartz!

Francine hands Jenna the hawk feather.

Francine: Here's a hawk feather, if you want it.

Muffy: We were trapped the entire time in that tacky gift shop. Fake fur and antlers everywhere!

Fern: You have every right to be mad, but it's not like we got lost on purpose.

In her imagination, she (as Fernfern), Francine (as the Commander) and Waggy are studying a map. A condor swoops down and pulls the map up, along with Francine who is holding on to it. Fern grabs Francine’s legs but has to let go. Francine drops to the ground.

Fern: There we were without a map.

Fern, Francine and Waggy ride a raft on a river. A crocodile attacks. Francine hits it with her paddle. It comes from the other side and grabs Fern’s shoe.

The fantasy ends. Prunella, Muffy and Jenna are listening intently.

Fern: True, I only lost a shoe when the deep, dark swamp tried to consume me, but were it not for brave Commander Francine, I would have surely perished in that bottomless bog.

The car drives down a road.

Muffy: Okay, okay, I'm sold. I want exclusive rights to the Moose Mountain story. It's definitely a book, but we sell the movie rights before it goes to press. Of course, we should start thinking serial right now!

The car passes a billboard advertising “Fernfern and the Secret of Moose Mountain”.
