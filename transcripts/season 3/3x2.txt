The Ballad of Buster Baxter
Mr. Read cuts the cake which is shaped like Buster’s head. The singing moose steps into the picture.

Singing Moose: (sings) This is the story 'bout the face on the cake / It belongs to Buster Baxter /

Flashback: Bitzi and baby Buster in the hospital. He starts eating the contents of her fruit basket.

Singing Moose: Baby boy bunny Buster Baxter was born eight years ago / At first, all he did was cry and eat / And eat, and eat, and grow / Eat and grow, that's all he did /

Flashback: Buster meets Arthur at the sandbox (from Episode 01-22b “Team Trouble”).

Singing Moose:He met Arthur, they became friends / It was an historic day! /

Flashback: Arthur and Buster ride their bikes.

Singing Moose:They always had lots of fun / They liked to read and play, hey! / Then one day, Buster found out he was gonna go...

Flashback: Arthur runs after the Baxters’ car.

Singing Moose: ...gonna go bye-bye /

Arthur: Goodbye!

Buster: Goodbye, Arthur!

Singing Moose: He went around the world a couple of times / We thought he left the show /

Buster sits in an airplane looking out of the window.

Singing Moose: Buster Baxter went away, then he came back / Buster Baxter, this is what he found.

Buster: It looks just like I never left!

Title Card: Magnifiying Glass
Buster and Arthur are playing checkers in Arthur’s room.

Buster: Mmm, great cake. I'm the only kid I know who got to eat his own face.

Arthur: That's exactly what I said!

Buster: Ah, it sure feels good to be back. I was afraid things would have changed around here while I was gone.

Arthur takes away the dishes as Brain comes in.

Brain: Hey, Buster! Glad you're back. I brought the scoreboard, Arthur.

Buster: Huh?

Arthur: While you were gone, we started a checker championship. Best of five thousand games.

Brain: So far, it's a tie. We're scheduled to finish today.

Arthur: Oh, I forgot.

Buster: Go ahead. I'll watch. There's only... He looks at the scorecard. ...seventy games left.

Arthur: But you just came home. There must be a way we can all play.

Singing Moose: "Arthur didn't wanna leave Buster out, of that he can be proud, / but when you're playing checkers… three's a crowd."

Arthur, Brain and Buster try to play three-way checkers.

Arthur: You can't move that black piece. That's my black piece.

Buster: No, it's my black piece.

Arthur: No. That one is mine, and that one is yours. You're jumping your own piece with my piece. It's easy. Brain is red and his pieces go this way. I'm black and go that way. Buster, you have some red pieces and some black, a penny, a stamp and the Monopoly shoe and you go across the board that way.

Brain: Wait, that's... Oh, my brain hurts.

Buster: Maybe I should just watch.

Arthur: Thanks, Buster. It's only because this is for the championship.

Buster: It's okay. It's just nice to be back with my friends again.

Arthur and Brain are absorbed in their game.

Brain: What'd you say?

***

Arthur and Brain are writing in the treehouse. Buster stops with his bike.

Singing Moose: "This is the next day. / That's all I have to say. Hey!"

Buster: Hey, Arthur, wanna come over and see my travel slides? He climbs up. Oh, hi, Brain. What are you guys doing?

Brain: Writing a thrilling adventure book.

Buster: Like our book?

Arthur: Sort of the same book. But not exactly like our book.

Buster: You and the Brain are writing our book?!

In his imagination, Binky guards chase after a coach driven by Arthur as Robin Hood and Brain as Friar Tuck (similar to the fantasy in “Arthur’s Faraway Friend”).

Binky: After them!

Arthur: We're a perfect team, Friar Brain.

Brain: I was able to rescue you, using a simple mathematical formula. Allow me to explain. He shows a drawing. This is a right triangle. Therefore, the angle of inclination and velocity of the rescue rope swing were easy to determine!

Binky: I'll get you yet!

(Arthur+Brain laugh)

The fantasy ends.

Buster: You changed everything!

Brain: Because some of your situations were a tad unbelievable.

Buster: But it was exciting!

Brain: Now it's logical and exciting.

Arthur: Buster, you weren't around. You can help now. We'll all do it.

He hands Buster a pencil.

Buster: I'll do a scene with pirates riding a pterosaurus to town to crush buildings.

A thought bubble shows the pirates on a dinosaur. Arthur and Brain look at him.

Brain: Um, no, dinosaurs and pirates never co-existed.

Arthur: Now they ride horses.

In Buster’s bubble, the dinosaur shrinks to a horse.

Buster: Horses don't crush buildings!

Brain: They can crush flowers.

The pirates ride their horse over a flowery meadow.

Singing Moose: "Buster Baxter went away and he came back. / He was starting to feel like things had changed..."

(Buster sighs)

***

Buster and his mom pass a movie theater with posters for “Kresblain 5” and “Carpet of Doom”.

Buster: “Carpet of Doom”! Wow! I wanna see that! I'll call Arthur.

Francine, Brain, Arthur and Muffy come out of the theater.

Francine: Hey, it's Buster!

Buster: You guys went to see “Carpet of Doom” without me?

Muffy: We didn't go to...

Arthur quickly steps in front of her.

Arthur: We forgot to call you. Sorry.

Muffy: I was about to say, we didn't go to “Carpet of Doom”.

Buster: (laughs) Come on! I know Arthur wouldn't go to a Kresblain the Merry Magician movie. Arthur looks guilty. You always said you hated Kresblain. He's so...blah!

Arthur: I used to kind of not like Kresblain, but Francine loaned me all the tapes and I caught Kresblamania!

He pulls up his pullover to reveal a Kresblain T-shirt.

(Buster gasps)

The Singing Moose appears on a Kresblain poster.

Singing Moose: "Kresblamania, it's insania. / How could Arthur catch Kresblamania?”

Mrs. Baxter: We have to go, honey.

Buster: Bye.

He leaves. The others look after him. All seem embarrassed.

Singing Moose: "Kresblamania, it's insania. / How could Buster not catch Kresblamania?”

Muffy: Did you hear that? He thinks he's so above Kresblain!

Francine: I think, going away turned Buster into an S-N-O-B.

Buster: No, you don't think... No! Really?

***

Buster comes into the school cafeteria with a tray. Muffy, Arthur, Francine, Binky and Brain sit together.

Muffy: He thinks he's better than us ‘cause he went to other countries where they eat snails and stuff.

Binky: Who wants to trade for a peanut butter and, er... more peanut butter? Buster comes up. You wouldn't like it. No snails.

Singing Moose: "Life can be tough as nails / when your friends think you're a guy who likes to eat snails."

Everybody looks embarrassed. Buster walks away.

***

The kids are doing a square dance in the gym. Buster clumsily dances with Prunella.

Singing Moose: "The kids forgot they learned to dance when Buster wasn't around. / But, boy, did Buster know it. / He couldn't do-si-do it."

Buster+Prunella: Whoa!

Buster knocks down Prunella. Brain catches her. Buster runs away.

Singing Moose: "He decided to get out before he knocked everybody down! / Change partners! / Promenade! / Quack like a duck!

Muffy: Look! He thinks he's too good for square dancing too.

***

Buster sees a bag of “Muffy & Friends' Scrumptious Smackeroodles” from “How the Cookie Crumbles” in a supermarket.

(Buster sighs)

***

Singing Moose: "Buster was feeling more left out with every passing day / Even when he found something cool..."

Arthur sits on a swing at school. Buster brings a key like the one in “Finders Key-Pers”.

Buster: Arthur! I found a weird key! Maybe it's the key to the city, or a sports car! When we find out, we can split it fifty-fifty.

Arthur looks at the key.

Arthur: That belongs to Mr. Morris. You better put it back.

Singing Moose: "Someone else had found it first when he was away."

***

The school bell rings. The kids run out.

Francine: Kresblain Club meeting at Muffy's house! Don't be late!

Arthur: You wanna come to the Kresblain club?

Buster makes a face.

Buster: No, thanks. Go ahead.

Arthur: Well...see ya.

He leaves. Muffy walks past Buster with her nose held high.

Muffy: Hm!

Buster looks sad.

***

Buster and Sue Ellen throw hoops on the school playground.

Sue Ellen: When my family used to travel a lot, I always felt out of place when I came home.

Buster: Yeah? That's exactly how I feel.

Sue Ellen: Kids think you're weird if you accidentally use French words like “C’est la vie”.

Buster: I was only in France a week.

Sue Ellen: A week? I was only there a day. You must be an expert.

Buster: I didn't learn any words. And I didn't eat any snails. But I watched TV.

Flashback: Buster lies on a hotel bed in Paris.

TV announcer: Voici "Le Lapin Bionique".

Flashback ends.

Buster: They have the same shows, but in French. (gasps) You know how Bionic Bunny always says...

Sue Ellen: I don't know much about TV.

Buster: Oh. You wanna play checkers?

Sue Ellen: I don't like checkers. See ya.

She leaves. Buster sadly leaves as well.

***

Muffy bangs a gavel for the Kresblain Club meeting. Arthur, Francine, Brain and the Singing Moose are present (The latter sits on the couch eating potato chips. Nobody pays any attention to him.)

Arthur: I wish Buster were here.

Francine: Why? The things we do aren't exciting enough for him. He's changed.

Brain: He didn't even want to write a book with me and Arthur.

Muffy: Because it had no snails in it! Face it, we have nothing in common with Buster anymore.

Arthur: Maybe we do. We’re still his friends. If he likes new stuff, maybe we'd like it too.

Muffy: I never thought of that.

Arthur: Why don't we throw a party and watch his travel slides. Then we'll see what he saw and at least we'll have that in common.

Francine: Yeah!

***

The kids are having a “Welcome Back Buster” party. Sue Ellen dances Limbo.

Sue Ellen: Wow!

Francine is dancing while Arthur stands sadly at the buffet.

Francine: Great idea, Arthur. This is fun.

Arthur: Didn't you notice, Francine?

Francine: Didn't I notice what?

Arthur: Buster isn't here!

Everybody stops and looks around. Binky looks under an armchair and finds Pal.

Binky: No.

Muffy: Well, that's it. He obviously doesn't want to be our friend anymore.

Brain: She must be right. Why else wouldn't he come to the party?

Meanwhile Buster is watching TV looking as if he was about to cry. The Singing Moose stands in the room.

Singing Moose: (cheerful tune:) "He's a sad, sad bunny. A sad, sad bunny. / TV isn't funny when you're a sad, sad bunny. Yee-ha!"

Buster: Hey! That's not very sad music.

Singing Moose: (sad tune:) "He's a sad, sad bunny. A sad, sad, sad, sad bunny..."

(Buster sighs)

Meanwhile back at the party.

Arthur: I can't believe he didn't come.

Muffy: What did he say when you invited him?

Arthur: I didn't invite him. Francine invited him.

Francine: I didn't invite him. Muffy invited him.

Muffy: I thought Brain invited him.

Brain turns off the music.

Brain: Weighing the facts using elementary deduction, I conclude that no one invited Buster to this party.

Binky: You think that's why he's not here?

They watch Buster’s travel slides together.

The slides show Buster at Mount Rushmore, in front of a cable car in the mountains, in front of the Sphinx, with a penguin, in front of the Empire State Building and in front of the Colosseum.

(Francine+Arthur laugh)
