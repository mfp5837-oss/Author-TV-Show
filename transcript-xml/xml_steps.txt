These are some of the find and replace I used as a guidline for the transcripts, most frequient ones I used, and my idea of how I wanted it to kinda look 

Step 1 Title card 
Find:^Title Card:\s*(.+)$ 
Replace: <titlecard>$1</titlecard>

Step 2 Charcter and their lines 
Find:^([A-Z][A-Za-z .'-]+):\s*(.+)$$  
Replace:
<line>
  <speaker>$1</speaker>
  <dialogue>$2</dialogue>
</line>

Step 3 Arthur narration lines (may not be pressent in all scripts)
Find:^([A-Z][A-Za-z]+)\s*\(narrator\):\s*(.+)$
Replace:
<narration>
  <speaker>$1</speaker>
  <dialogue>$2</dialogue>
</narration>

Step 4 Scene setting (may be different for each script)
Find:^(In|At)\s+([^,]+)(.*)$
Replace: 
<scene>
  <location>$2</location>
  <description>$3</description>
</scene>

Step 5 Sounds (may be different for each script)
Find:\((burps|grunts|groans|laughs?|screams?|cheers?)\)
Replace:<sfx>$1</sfx>

Step 6 Stage direction
Find:^([A-Z][^.:\n]+?\.)$ or try this ^\[(.+?)\]$
Replace:<stage>$1</stage>

Step 7 Context (This may not be present in every script
Example:The rat (who is revealed to be Mr. Ratburn in a later episode) addresses his class.
Find:^\s*The rat \(who is revealed to be Mr\. Ratburn in a later episode\) addresses his class\.\s*$
Replace:<context>The rat (who is revealed to be Mr. Ratburn in a later episode) addresses his class.</context>
